# Trading AI Landscape (Goldman Sachs)

## 1. Product Scope

Trading AI at Goldman focuses on:

- Execution optimization
- Liquidity prediction
- Market impact modeling
- Order routing strategies
- Risk-aware execution

---

## 2. Core User

- Equity traders
- Execution desks
- Quant trading teams
- Portfolio execution teams

---

## 3. Core Problem

Large institutional trades suffer from:

- Market impact
- Timing inefficiencies
- Liquidity uncertainty
- Execution fragmentation across venues

Even small inefficiencies translate into large financial costs.

---

## 4. Traditional Systems

Existing systems rely on:

- VWAP / TWAP strategies
- Rule-based execution logic
- Historical heuristics
- Static routing logic

These approaches lack:

- Real-time adaptation
- Context awareness
- Predictive intelligence

---

## 5. AI Opportunity

### 5.1 Liquidity Prediction Models
- Predict short-term liquidity windows
- Identify optimal execution timing

---

### 5.2 Market Impact Models
- Estimate price impact of large trades
- Optimize execution schedules

---

### 5.3 Adaptive Execution Agents
- Adjust strategy based on market conditions
- Dynamic routing across venues

---

### 5.4 Execution Copilot
- Explain execution strategy
- Recommend trade-offs
- Provide real-time guidance

---

## 6. Human-in-the-Loop Requirement

Full automation is not acceptable.

System must:
- Recommend strategies
- Allow trader override
- Explain reasoning
- Log decisions for audit

---

## 7. Strategic Insight

Trading AI is fundamentally about:

> Minimizing execution cost while preserving trader control and market awareness.
