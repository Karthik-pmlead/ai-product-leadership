# Wealth Advisor Copilot

## Risks and Compliance Framework

---

# 1. Executive Summary

Wealth AI systems operate in a highly regulated environment where risks extend beyond model accuracy into:

* fiduciary responsibility
* suitability constraints
* regulatory compliance
* client trust

---

# 2. Risk Categories

---

# 2.1 Suitability & Fiduciary Risk

### Risks:

* inappropriate investment recommendations
* mismatch between risk profile and advice
* violation of fiduciary duty

### Mitigation:

* strict client risk profiling system
* suitability validation engine
* recommendation pre-checks

---

# 2.2 Hallucination Risk in Financial Advice

### Risks:

* incorrect financial guidance
* fabricated product recommendations
* misleading portfolio insights

### Mitigation:

* RAG-only grounded responses
* restricted knowledge sources
* citation-enforced outputs

---

# 2.3 Regulatory Compliance Risk

### Risks:

* violation of financial advisory regulations
* lack of auditability
* non-compliant communication

### Mitigation:

* full advisory audit logs
* policy enforcement engine
* compliance pre-approval workflows

---

# 2.4 Data Privacy Risk

### Risks:

* leakage of sensitive client financial data
* unauthorized advisor access
* cross-client data contamination

### Mitigation:

* strict RBAC controls
* client-level data isolation
* encryption and secure retrieval systems

---

# 2.5 Advisor Over-Reliance Risk

### Risks:

* blind trust in AI recommendations
* reduced human judgment in advisory decisions

### Mitigation:

* AI as advisory assistant only (not decision authority)
* mandatory advisor review steps
* explainable recommendation outputs

---

# 3. Governance Framework

---

## 3.1 Compliance-by-Design

All AI outputs must:

* be traceable
* be explainable
* be grounded in verified financial data

---

## 3.2 Auditability

Every recommendation logs:

* client context
* data sources used
* reasoning steps
* advisor approval actions

---

## 3.3 Human Oversight Model

* advisor remains final decision authority
* high-risk recommendations require additional approval
* automated suggestions always require review

---

# 4. Safe AI Design Principles

* no autonomous investment execution
* no unsupervised portfolio changes
* no cross-client data leakage
* no ungrounded financial advice

---

# 5. Strategic Insight

> In wealth AI, trust is the product. Compliance is the architecture.

---

# 6. Final Recommendation

Adopt a **“compliance-native AI architecture”** where:

* intelligence is maximized within regulatory bounds
* advisor authority is preserved
* every recommendation is auditable and explainable
* AI augments, never replaces fiduciary responsibility
