# Explainable AI in Finance

In financial systems, explainability is not a feature — it is a requirement.

---

## Core Principle

An AI system is only useful in finance if:

> Every output can be traced, justified, and audited.

---

## Why Explainability Matters

Financial decisions involve:
- regulatory constraints
- high capital exposure
- reputational risk

Therefore, black-box predictions are unacceptable.

---

## Explainability Dimensions

### 1. Source Traceability
- Every insight must link to underlying data or research

### 2. Reasoning Transparency
- Step-by-step logic behind conclusions

### 3. Uncertainty Representation
- Confidence scoring and scenario divergence

### 4. Alternative Perspectives
- Showing non-consensus views

---

## Strategic Insight

Explainability is not post-processing.

It must be built into:

> retrieval → reasoning → output generation

---

## Key Shift

From:
> “What did the model predict?”

To:
> “Why should I trust this decision?”
