# 01_problem_and_context

# Problem Statement

## Business context
The business wants to help users discover the most relevant items faster, such as products, content, support articles, or actions.

## Problem
Users often face too many choices, which makes discovery inefficient and reduces conversion, engagement, or task completion.

## Current pain
Without recommendations, users search manually, browse too much, or miss relevant items entirely.

## Business impact
Poor discovery leads to lower engagement, weaker conversion, and a less useful user experience.

## Desired outcome
Build a recommender that presents relevant items to users based on their behavior, context, or preferences.

---
# User Research

## Primary users

| User group | What they need | How they use the output |
|---|---|---|
| End users | Find relevant items quickly. | View recommended items and choose faster. |
| Product managers | Increase engagement, conversion, or retention. | Use recommendation performance to prioritize product changes. |
| Growth / marketing teams | Improve discovery and upsell. | Use recommendations in campaigns or surfaces. |
| Operations / content teams | Surface the most relevant items. | Tune item catalog or content strategy. |

## Key research questions
- What do users want to discover?
- What action matters most: click, purchase, read, save, or complete?
- Where should recommendations appear?
- How much personalization is useful?
- What is the biggest discovery friction today?

## What success looks like
- Users find relevant items faster.
- Engagement improves.
- The recommender feels useful rather than annoying.

---

# Assumptions

- User behavior data exists or can be collected.
- There are enough items to recommend.
- Item metadata is available.
- The business has a clear definition of success.
- A first version can start with one surface or use case.

---
# Constraints

- New users may have little or no history.
- New items may have little or no interaction data.
- Recommendations must be fast enough for the product experience.
- The system should avoid obvious bias or repetition.
- The first version should be scoped to one use case or surface.
