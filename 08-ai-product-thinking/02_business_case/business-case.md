# Business Case

## Opportunity

Customer feedback is spread across surveys, reviews, tickets, and comments, which makes it difficult to understand what customers feel about specific product and service aspects. An aspect sentiment capability can transform unstructured feedback into structured insight about themes such as quality, delivery, support, pricing, and usability. Customer sentiment tools are commonly used to analyze feedback at scale and surface issue themes that matter to the business. [web:60][web:61][web:66]

## Business problem

Today, feedback is reviewed manually or summarized at a high level, so teams often know that customers are unhappy but not exactly why. This creates slower response times, weaker prioritization, and more guesswork in product and operations decisions. Aspect-based sentiment analysis is designed to break feedback into specific aspects and evaluate sentiment for each one, which makes it better suited to this problem than overall sentiment alone. [web:59][web:60]

## Business value

| Value area | Impact |
|---|---|
| Product prioritization | Identify which aspects drive positive or negative customer experience. |
| Customer support | Surface recurring issues faster and improve escalation handling. |
| Operations | Spot service problems such as delivery delays or fulfillment failures. |
| CX reporting | Replace manual tagging with scalable, repeatable analysis. |
| Executive visibility | Track customer sentiment trends by aspect over time. |

The business value comes from turning raw feedback into actions that reduce customer pain and support better decisions. Companies use sentiment analysis to optimize customer care work, prioritize urgent issues, and make management decisions based on more objective feedback patterns. [web:60][web:55][web:65]

## Initial pilot scope

The first pilot should focus on a narrow and useful set of aspects:
- product quality
- delivery
- customer support
- pricing
- usability

The first release should analyze one or two major feedback channels, such as support tickets and survey comments, rather than trying to cover every source at once. A limited scope reduces complexity while still delivering visible business value. [web:61][web:66]

## Success metrics

| Metric | Example target |
|---|---|
| Aspect classification coverage | 80%+ of comments mapped to at least one aspect |
| Sentiment accuracy | Enough to support trend analysis and prioritization |
| Manual effort reduction | Less time spent reading and tagging comments |
| Time to insight | Faster weekly or monthly reporting |
| Actionability | Teams use the output in product or CX reviews |

The goal is not perfect language understanding on day one. The goal is to produce reliable, repeatable insight that changes how teams make decisions. [web:59][web:60]

## Cost and effort

The main costs are data preparation, taxonomy design, model evaluation, workflow integration, and maintenance. Because this is an insight-generation use case, the pilot can be lightweight and still produce value before full automation is considered. For a business case, this is attractive because the output can be validated quickly against real customer feedback. [web:64][web:66]

## Recommendation

This use case is worth pursuing because it is easy to explain, measurable, and directly tied to customer experience improvement. A first pilot should prove that the organization can reliably identify the aspects customers talk about most and whether those aspects trend positive or negative over time. [web:60][web:62]
