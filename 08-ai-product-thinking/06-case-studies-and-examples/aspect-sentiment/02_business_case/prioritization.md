# Prioritization

## Purpose
Decide whether aspect sentiment analysis should be the first AI use case to pursue.

## Prioritization criteria

| Criterion | Weight | Score guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Business value | High | Does it improve a real business decision or workflow? |
| Data readiness | High | Is the needed feedback available now? |
| Implementation complexity | Medium | Can we build a first version without major dependencies? |
| Time to value | High | Can the pilot show results quickly? |
| Operational fit | Medium | Will teams actually use the output? |
| Risk | High | Is the use case low enough risk for a pilot? |

## Example scoring

| Use case | Business value | Data readiness | Complexity | Time to value | Overall |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Aspect sentiment analysis | High | High | Medium | High | Strong candidate |
| Recommendation system | High | Medium | Medium-High | Medium | Later candidate |
| Customer support triage | High | Medium | Medium | High | Strong candidate |
| Document classification | Medium | High | Low | High | Good candidate |

## Why aspect sentiment ranks well
- It is easy to explain to business users.
- It addresses a real and common customer experience problem.
- It can be scoped to a narrow taxonomy.
- It creates visible output for product, CX, and support teams.
- It supports both insight and operational improvement.

## Prioritization conclusion
Aspect sentiment analysis should be prioritized as an early AI use case because it balances value, feasibility, and speed. It is strong enough to justify a pilot and simple enough to show meaningful results early. [web:70][web:72][web:75][web:79]
