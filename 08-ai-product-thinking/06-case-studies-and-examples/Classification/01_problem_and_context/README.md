# Problem and Context

## Problem statement
Sales operations teams need to classify and route incoming leads, requests, emails, or CRM items quickly, but manual triage is slow, inconsistent, and hard to scale.

## User context
The use case is typically used by sales operations, sales development, account teams, or operations teams that handle incoming work and need to prioritize it correctly.

## Assumptions
- The business has a repeatable stream of incoming sales items.
- A taxonomy for category, urgency, or ownership can be defined.
- Historical examples or labels exist, or can be created.
- Human review is available for uncertain cases.

## Constraints
- Incoming items may be short, noisy, or incomplete.
- The triage logic must be fast enough for operational use.
- The system should not make irreversible decisions on low-confidence items.
- Sensitive customer or deal data must be protected.

## Why this matters
Sales triage is only useful if the labels are consistent, the routing is trusted, and the workflow reduces manual sorting rather than adding complexity. [web:264][web:265][web:270]
