# Business Case

## Opportunity
Recommendations can improve discovery and help users find more relevant items faster.

## Business problem
Users are overwhelmed by choice and need help identifying the most relevant next item.

## Business value
- Higher click-through rate.
- Better conversion.
- Improved retention.
- More content or product discovery.

## Recommendation
Start with a narrow recommender use case and measure lift on a single surface before scaling.

---
# ROI Model

## Value drivers
- More clicks.
- More purchases or completions.
- Better engagement.
- Improved retention.

## Cost categories
- Data preparation.
- Model development.
- Infrastructure and serving.
- Experimentation and monitoring.
- Ongoing model refresh.

## ROI view
The recommender should pay back through measurable lift in user engagement or conversion.

---
# Feasibility Assessment

## Checkpoints
- Interaction data available.
- Catalog or content metadata available.
- Feedback loop can be measured.
- Latency is acceptable.
- New-user and cold-start strategy exists.

## Conclusion
A recommender is feasible if the business has enough interaction data and a clear surface to optimize.

---
# Prioritization

## Criteria
- Business impact.
- Data readiness.
- Implementation complexity.
- Time to value.
- Experimentability.

## Conclusion
This use case is strong if the business has high traffic and measurable user actions.
