# Risks & Assumptions

---

# Executive Summary

Enterprise AI products in financial services require careful consideration of technology, regulation, data governance, security, and user trust.

This document outlines the primary risks associated with building and operating an AI-powered investment banking workspace.

---

# Business Risks

## Low User Adoption

Risk

Users continue using existing tools.

Mitigation

- Pilot with high-value workflows
- Executive sponsorship
- Training
- Usage analytics

---

## Change Management

Risk

Users resist AI-driven workflows.

Mitigation

- Human-in-the-loop
- Incremental rollout
- Clear explainability

---

# AI Risks

## Hallucinations

Risk

AI produces inaccurate financial information.

Mitigation

- Retrieval-Augmented Generation
- Source citations
- Confidence scoring
- Human approval

---

## Prompt Injection

Mitigation

- Input validation
- Policy enforcement
- Secure prompt orchestration

---

## Model Drift

Mitigation

- Continuous evaluation
- Benchmark testing
- Prompt versioning

---

# Data Risks

- Incomplete datasets
- Duplicate entities
- Outdated research
- Incorrect relationships

Mitigation

Master Data Management

Data quality monitoring

Automated validation

---

# Security Risks

- Unauthorized access
- Insider threats
- API abuse
- Data leakage

Mitigation

- RBAC
- Encryption
- Audit logs
- Zero Trust Architecture

---

# Regulatory Risks

Applicable regulations may include:

- GDPR
- Regional privacy laws
- Financial services regulations
- Internal governance policies

Mitigation

- Data classification
- Consent management
- Retention policies
- Compliance reviews

---

# Technology Risks

- Third-party API outages
- LLM provider changes
- Infrastructure failures
- Performance bottlenecks

Mitigation

- Multi-provider architecture
- Caching
- Circuit breakers
- Observability

---

# Product Assumptions

The strategy assumes:

- Users trust AI with citations
- Market data integrations are available
- Existing workflows remain broadly consistent
- Executive sponsorship exists
- AI improves productivity without replacing expert judgment

---

# Success Criteria

The platform succeeds when:

- AI is trusted
- Data is reliable
- Users adopt the platform
- Security is maintained
- Regulatory obligations are satisfied
