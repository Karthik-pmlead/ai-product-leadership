# Product Metrics

---

# Measurement Philosophy

The objective is not simply to measure usage but to understand whether the platform improves decision quality, productivity, and business outcomes.

Metrics are organized into five categories.

---

# North Star Metric

Average Time to Decision

Definition:

Time required for a banker to move from identifying a business question to taking an informed action.

Examples include preparing for a client meeting, evaluating a company, or qualifying a deal opportunity.

A reduction in this metric indicates the platform is effectively surfacing trusted information and accelerating decision-making.

# Success Targets (Illustrative)

Within the first year:

- 80% weekly active usage among pilot bankers
- 40% reduction in research time
- 50% reduction in meeting preparation time
- 70% AI Copilot adoption
- 95% search success rate
- 99.9% platform availability
- Less than 2-second median response time

---

# Continuous Improvement

Metrics should inform quarterly roadmap reviews, helping prioritize improvements based on user value, business impact, and operational performance rather than feature delivery alone.
