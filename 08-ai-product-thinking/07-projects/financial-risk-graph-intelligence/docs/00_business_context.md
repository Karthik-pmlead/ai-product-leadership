
# 00 - Business Context

## Overview

Modern financial systems are highly interconnected.

Banks, hedge funds, exchanges, traders, and institutions continuously interact through:
- transactions
- counterparty relationships
- liquidity dependencies
- market exposure

A failure or risk event in one entity can propagate rapidly through the financial ecosystem.

---

## Problem Statement

Traditional tabular monitoring systems struggle to:
- model hidden relationships
- identify contagion effects
- detect systemic concentration risks
- explain interconnected exposure

Financial institutions require:
- real-time intelligence
- graph-based risk visibility
- explainable risk propagation
- anomaly detection capabilities

---

## Business Need

Organizations need platforms capable of:
- modeling interconnected entities
- tracking dynamic risk changes
- detecting suspicious patterns
- monitoring systemic exposure
- generating explainable alerts

---

## Industry Relevance

This type of system is relevant for:
- investment banks
- stock exchanges
- hedge funds
- compliance teams
- fraud intelligence groups

---

## Key Use Cases

### 1. Counterparty Risk Monitoring
Identify exposure chains across institutions.

### 2. Fraud Detection
Detect suspiciously connected entities.

### 3. Market Surveillance
Monitor abnormal trading relationship patterns.

### 4. Systemic Risk Analysis
Understand contagion impact propagation.

### 5. News-Driven Intelligence
Update risk dynamically based on market news.
