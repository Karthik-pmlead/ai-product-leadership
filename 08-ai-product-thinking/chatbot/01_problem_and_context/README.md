# Problem and Context

## Problem statement
Users need fast, conversational help to answer questions, complete tasks, or navigate information, but manual support or search is slow and inconsistent.

## User context
The chatbot will likely be used by customers, employees, or support teams in a workflow where quick answers matter more than deep exploration.

## Assumptions
- The use case has a bounded domain.
- Relevant knowledge sources exist.
- The business can define acceptable answer quality.
- Human escalation is available when needed.

## Constraints
- The chatbot must avoid unsafe or incorrect answers.
- Latency should be low enough for conversational use.
- Sensitive or private information must be protected.
- The first version should focus on one domain or workflow.

## Why this matters
A chatbot is only useful if the problem is narrow enough to answer well and safe enough to trust. [web:207][web:210][web:213]
