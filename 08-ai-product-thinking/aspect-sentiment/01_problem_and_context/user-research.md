# User Research

## Primary users

| User group | What they need | How they use the output |
|---|---|---|
| Product managers | Understand which features or product aspects create positive or negative sentiment. | Prioritize roadmap decisions and fix high-impact issues. |
| Customer support leaders | Identify recurring complaint themes and service pain points. | Improve support workflows and training. |
| CX / VoC teams | Analyze customer feedback at scale. | Build dashboards, trend reports, and action plans. |
| Operations leaders | Detect operational issues such as delivery delays or fulfillment problems. | Escalate process improvements. |
| Business leaders | See whether improvements are moving customer sentiment. | Track business health and customer experience trends. |

## Key research questions
- What aspects matter most to customers?
- Which aspects are most often negative?
- Which feedback sources are most valuable?
- How quickly do teams need insight?
- What decisions will be made from the analysis?

## Observed user needs
- Users want a quick summary of what is working and what is not.
- Users need aspect-level detail, not just overall sentiment.
- Users want trends over time and by channel.
- Users need outputs that are easy to trust and explain.

## Workflow today
1. Feedback is collected from multiple channels.
2. Someone manually reads or samples comments.
3. A summary is created in slides or spreadsheets.
4. Teams react late or focus on the loudest issues rather than the most common ones.

## What success looks like
- Faster insight into recurring issues.
- Better prioritization of product and service improvements.
- Higher confidence in customer feedback analysis.
- Less manual effort in reading and tagging comments.
