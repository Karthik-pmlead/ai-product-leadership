# ROI Model

## Objective
Estimate the business value of aspect sentiment analysis by comparing expected benefits with implementation and operating costs.

## Value drivers

| Value driver | How it creates value |
|---|---|
| Faster insight | Reduces time spent manually reading and tagging customer feedback. |
| Better prioritization | Helps teams focus on the most frequent or most negative aspects. |
| Improved CX actions | Makes it easier to address recurring issues faster. |
| Better reporting | Standardizes customer feedback analysis across teams. |
| Reduced manual work | Lowers effort for analysts and operations teams. |

## Cost categories

| Cost category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Data preparation | Feedback consolidation, cleaning, taxonomy design, labeling. |
| Model work | Baseline model setup, evaluation, tuning, and iteration. |
| Integration | Dashboard or workflow integration, export logic, APIs. |
| Human review | Reviewer time for ambiguous cases and quality checks. |
| Ongoing operations | Monitoring, maintenance, taxonomy updates, and retraining. |

## Simple ROI formula
\(ROI = \frac{Benefits - Costs}{Costs}\)

## Example benefit logic
If the team saves analyst time, improves prioritization, and reduces recurring customer issues, the total value can exceed implementation cost quickly. Sentiment analysis ROI is often strongest when it is tied to workflow improvement and customer experience decisions rather than treated as a standalone model. [web:67][web:77][web:80]

## Scenario view

| Scenario | Assumption | Expected result |
|---|---|---|
| Conservative | Limited automation and modest adoption | Value comes mainly from time savings and better reporting. |
| Base case | Solid taxonomy and consistent usage | Stronger operational and CX value. |
| Upside | Broad team adoption and strong trend visibility | Higher business impact through better prioritization and faster action. |

## ROI conclusion
The use case can deliver good ROI because it is repeatable, measurable, and relatively low risk compared with more automated customer-facing AI systems. The business value is strongest when the outputs feed into regular product and CX decisions. [web:67][web:71][web:77]
