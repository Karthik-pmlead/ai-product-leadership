# Case Interview Frameworks Reference Guide

A comprehensive, production-ready reference matrix mapping classic management consulting frameworks. This guide is structured as a Mutual Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive (MECE) playbook for product managers, strategy operators, and consultants to diagnose business problems, optimize profitability, and evaluate market entries.

---

## 🚀 How to Navigate This Repository

1. **Initial Assessment Phase:** Start with **`5c.md`**, **`mece.md`**, or **`porters_five_forces.md`** to structure your problem space without bias.
2. **Financial Deep Dives:** Use **`profitability.md`** to isolate variances, then map the operations via **`value_chain_analysis.md`** or **`red_costs.md`** to pinpoint leakage.
3. **Strategic Trajectory:** Map your expansion opportunities using the **`ansoff_matrix.md`**, choose monetization strategies using **`pricing.md`**, and balance corporate divisions using **`bcg_growthshare.md`**.
*Maintained for Strategy Operators and Product Managers. Contributions, edge cases, and modern tech case studies are welcome.*

## 🛠️ Core Frameworks Reference Matrix

| Framework Category | Strategic Objective | Core Pillars / Components | Key Diagnostic Questions | Implementation Notes & Context |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **MECE** *(Core Principle)* | Problem Structuring | • Mutually Exclusive<br>• Collectively Exhaustive | • Are there any overlapping structural categories?<br>• Do all branches cover 100% of the problem space? | *The foundation for all case structuring. Ensure no logic gaps exist in your root-cause analysis issue trees.* |
| **Profitability** | Financial Optimization & Diagnostics | • Revenue (Price × Quantity)<br>• Costs (Fixed Costs + Variable Costs) | • Has revenue declined, or have costs escalated?<br>• Which specific product line or segment drives the variance? | *Isolate problems by volume, pricing tier, or operational cost structures before drilling down into underlying drivers.* |
| **Reducing Costs** | Margin Improvement | • Internal (Labor, Materials, Overhead)<br>• External (Vendor rates, Regulatory, Logistics) | • Can we renegotiate supplier contracts?<br>• Where can automation eliminate operational inefficiencies? | *Analyze labor utilization, production bottlenecks, capacity constraints, and supply chain redundancies.* |
| **Industry Analysis** | Market Dynamics Assessment | • Market Size & Growth Rate<br>• Competitor Landscapes<br>• Industry Drivers & Margins | • Is the industry expanding, consolidating, or stagnating?<br>• What are the barriers to entry and exit? | *Provides macro environmental context. Pairs naturally with micro-economic deep dives.* |
| **Developing New Product** | Product Innovation & Launch | • Product Differentiation<br>• Cannibalization Risk<br>• Pricing Strategy & R&D | • Does this product address an unmet user need?<br>• Will it cannibalize our existing high-margin portfolio? | *Crucial for tech Product Managers. Focus heavily on market fit, UX validation, engineering complexity, and MVP roadmaps.* |
| **Entering a New Market** | Strategic Growth & Expansion | • Market Attractiveness<br>• Entry Modes (Organic, JV, Acquisition)<br>• Financial Feasibility | • What is the Total Addressable Market (TAM)?<br>• Should we build, buy, or partner to capture this market? | *Assess regulatory barriers, cultural alignment, localized competitor responses, and CapEx payback timelines.* |
| **Mergers & Acquisitions (M&A)** | Inorganic Growth Evaluation | • Strategic Fit & Synergies<br>• Valuation & Financials<br>• Integration Risks | • What cost or revenue synergies justify the premium?<br>• How complex is the post-merger cultural and tech integration? | *Evaluate exit strategies, due diligence checklists, legal/regulatory roadblocks, and operational compatibility.* |
| **Pricing Strategy** | Monetization & Value Capture | • Cost-Based Pricing<br>• Competitive-Based Pricing<br>• Value-Based Pricing | • What is the economic value to the customer (EVC)?<br>• How will competitors react to our pricing tiers? | *For software/tech, prioritize Value-Based Pricing, pricing elasticities, and recurring SaaS models over Cost-Based.* |
| **Growth Strategies** | Market & Portfolio Expansion | • Increase Distribution Channels<br>• Diversify Product Lines<br>• Acquire Competitors | • Can we cross-sell existing products to current segments?<br>• Which new customer acquisition channels are untapped? | *Focuses broadly on top-line acceleration. Map specific initiatives across corporate risk thresholds.* |
| **Competitive Response** | Market Defense & Counter-Strategy | • Direct Defense (Match Price/Product)<br>• Indirect Defense (Pivot/Cross-sell)<br>• Strategic Acquisition | • Is the competitor's move a long-term threat or a short-term blip?<br>• What is our unique, non-replicable value proposition? | *Analyze the game-theoretic impact of your counter-moves on overall industry pricing stability and market share.* |
| **Increasing Sales** | Top-line Revenue Generation | • Volume Growth (New users/segments)<br>• Transaction Size (Up-selling)<br>• Frequency (Retention) | • How can we optimize our marketing conversion funnel?<br>• What retention strategies can increase customer lifetime value (LTV)? | *Focus on operational levers within the B2B sales pipeline or digital acquisition funnels: Lead Gen → Convert → Retain.* |
| **The 5 C's** | Holistic Business Diagnosis | • Company, Competitors, Customers, Collaborators, Context | • What are our core competencies?<br>• How are macroeconomic trends shifting consumer habits? | *An excellent initial framing tool to rapidly orient yourself in highly ambiguous, multi-dimensional corporate settings.* |
| **The 4 P's** | Marketing Mix Operationalization | • Product, Price, Place, Promotion | • Is our marketing message aligned with our target persona?<br>• Are distribution logistics optimizing product availability? | *Tactical implementation framework. Best applied after defining customer segmentation, target personas, and brand positioning.* |
| **Porter's Five Forces**  | Structural Attractiveness & Industry Profitability | • Supplier Power, Buyer Power, Substitutes, New Entrants, Rivalry | • Can suppliers squeeze our operational margins?<br>• Is there a high risk of cross-industry technological substitution? | *Deep micro-economic tool to assess long-term sector profitability and structural barriers to entry.* |
| **Ansoff Matrix** | Targeted Strategic Growth Vectors | • Market Penetration, Market Development, Product Development, Diversification | • Are we maximizing penetration in our current core markets?<br>• Does moving into a new quadrant introduce unacceptable risk? | *Categorizes growth ideas onto a clean 2x2 grid based on product-market familiarity to balance corporate risk.* |
| **BCG Growth-Share Matrix**  | Corporate Portfolio Resource Allocation | • Stars (High growth, High share)<br>• Cash Cows<br>• Question Marks<br>• Dogs | • Which business units should fund future bets?<br>• Are we wasting precious capital sustaining negative-margin 'Dogs'? | *Essential for multi-product corporations, conglomerates, and Private Equity sizing up resource distribution.* |
| **Value Chain Analysis** | Internal Cost & Margin Optimization | • Primary (Logistics, Ops, Sales)<br>• Support (HR, Tech/R&D, Procurement) | • Where exactly within primary operations are we losing margins?<br>• Can support activities be shared to drive efficiencies? | *Traces a product linearly from raw R&D/materials to customer delivery. Outstanding for identifying operational leaks.* |
