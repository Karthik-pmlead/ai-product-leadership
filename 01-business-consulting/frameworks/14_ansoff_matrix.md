# Ansoff Matrix (Growth Vector Analysis)

## Overview
The Ansoff Matrix is a classic 2x2 strategic planning tool that categorizes growth vectors based on corporate familiarity with the target product and market. It directly aligns growth ideas with their associated operational risk profiles.

## The 2x2 Matrix Grid

| | Existing Products | New Products |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Existing Markets** | **Market Penetration**<br>• *Risk:* Lowest<br>• Focus: Increase market share, optimize conversion, loyalty programs. | **Product Development**<br>• *Risk:* Medium<br>• Focus: R&D, cross-selling new features, launch MVP innovations. |
| **New Markets** | **Market Development**<br>• *Risk:* Medium<br>• Focus: Geographic expansion, target new customer personas/demographics. | **Diversification**<br>• *Risk:* Highest<br>• Focus: Unrelated business units, vertical/horizontal integration. |

## Quadrant Breakdown & Levers

### 1. Market Penetration (Existing Market / Existing Product)
* **Strategy:** Sell more of the current portfolio to the core user base.
* **Levers:** Lower prices, accelerate marketing, optimize customer acquisition cost (CAC), launch retention campaigns.

### 2. Product Development (Existing Market / New Product)
* **Strategy:** Create new solutions or lines for customers who already trust your brand.
* **Levers:** Invest in R&D, bundle peripheral products, acquire product tech to integrate into your existing distribution channel.

### 3. Market Development (New Market / Existing Product)
* **Strategy:** Take your successful product and introduce it to completely fresh contexts.
* **Levers:** International expansion, repackaging corporate B2C products for B2B mid-market accounts, alternative sales channels.

### 4. Diversification (New Market / New Product)
* **Strategy:** Enter a completely new industry with a brand new product.
* **Levers:** *Conglomerate diversification* (unrelated sectors) or *Concentric diversification* (technologically unrelated but sharing operational logic).

## Key Case Interview Questions
* Can our current sales force naturally cross-sell this proposed new product, or will it require a costly separate distribution channel?
* Given our low risk tolerance, should we prioritize deep Market Penetration over risky Diversification?
