# Build vs Buy Framework

## Objective
Framework for evaluating whether to build internally, buy externally, or partner for a business capability, product, platform, or technology solution.

---

# Decision Overview

| Option | Definition |
|---|---|
| Build | Develop internally |
| Buy | Purchase external solution |
| Partner | Strategic integration or alliance |

---

# Evaluation Criteria

| Criteria | Build | Buy | Partner |
|---|---|---|---|
| Time to Market | Slow | Fast | Medium |
| Cost | High upfront | Subscription/license | Shared |
| Customization | High | Limited | Medium |
| Maintenance | Internal burden | Vendor-managed | Shared |
| Strategic Control | Full | Limited | Partial |

---

# Strategic Questions

## Core Business Alignment
- Is this capability strategically differentiating?
- Does it create competitive advantage?
- Is ownership important?

---

## Time to Market
- How urgent is deployment?
- Is speed critical to revenue?
- Are competitors moving quickly?

---

## Resource Availability
- Do we have internal expertise?
- Engineering bandwidth available?
- Long-term support capability?

# Recommendation Framework

## Build If
- Core strategic capability
- Long-term differentiation matters
- Strong internal engineering team
- Custom workflows required

---

## Buy If
- Commodity capability
- Fast deployment required
- Limited engineering resources
- Proven vendor ecosystem exists

---

## Partner If
- Shared market opportunity
- Ecosystem expansion beneficial
- Strategic integration valuable
