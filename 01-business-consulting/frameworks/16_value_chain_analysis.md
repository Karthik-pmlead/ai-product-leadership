# Value Chain Analysis

## Overview
Pioneered by Michael Porter, Value Chain Analysis disassembles an enterprise into its discrete primary and support activities to trace how value is sequentially added and where margin leakage occurs. It is an invaluable framework for deep cost diagnostic and operational optimization cases.

## The Value Chain Structural Blueprint

```
 ┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │ SUPPORT ACTIVITIES:                                                       │
 │  • Firm Infrastructure (Finance, Legal, Exec Management)                 │
 │  • Human Resource Management (Recruiting, Training, Compensation)         │
 │  • Technology & R&D (Product Design, Software Dev, Process Engineering)   │
 │  • Procurement (Vendor Sourcing, Component Purchasing, Contract Admin)    │
 └─────────────────────────────────────┬─────────────────────────────────────┘
                                       │ (Enables Primary Actions)
                                       ▼
 ┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │ PRIMARY ACTIVITIES:                                                       │
 │  Inbound   ───►  Operations  ───►  Outbound  ───► Marketing ───► Service  │
 │  Logistics      (Manufacturing,    Logistics      & Sales      (Support,   │
 │ (Receiving,      Assembly, QA)    (Fulfillment,  (Pricing,     Warranty)  │
 │  Inventory)                        Shipping)      Channels)               │
 └───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Activity Breakdowns

### Primary Activities (Direct Product Flow)
1.  **Inbound Logistics:** Handling, storing, and managing raw materials and component inventory arriving from suppliers.
2.  **Operations:** Transforming inputs directly into the final product or service (machining, packaging, testing, cloud infrastructure deployment).
3.  **Outbound Logistics:** Storing, distributing, and shipping the finished product or asset to wholesalers, retailers, or end-users.
4.  **Marketing & Sales:** Activities used to locate, educate, and persuade target buyers to purchase (pricing strategy, ad spend, enterprise sales loops).
5.  **Service:** Enhancing or maintaining product value post-purchase (customer success, warranty repairs, technical support desk).

### Support Activities (The Infrastructure Layer)
* **Procurement:** Negotiating contract terms and buying the physical inputs used across the entire chain.
* **Technology Development:** R&D, product design, automated workflows, and internal tooling that improve product quality or lower production costs.
* **HR Management:** Hiring, retaining, and setting incentives for skilled engineers, operational staff, and leadership teams.
* **Infrastructure:** General corporate governance, accounting systems, regulatory compliance, and legal safeguards.

## Key Case Interview Questions
* Where exactly along the primary sequence is our cost per unit spiking compared to industry benchmarks?
* Can outsourcing our Outbound Logistics to a third-party vendor instantly reclaim lost profit margins?
