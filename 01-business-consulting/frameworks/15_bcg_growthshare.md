# BCG Growth-Share Matrix

## Overview
Created by the Boston Consulting Group, this matrix helps multi-divisional corporations evaluate portfolio business units based on market attractiveness (Market Growth Rate) and competitive position (Relative Market Share). It drives data-backed corporate budgeting and resource allocation.

## The Matrix Model

| | High Relative Market Share | Low Relative Market Share |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **High Market Growth** | **STARS**<br>• Strategy: Invest heavily.<br>• Profile: High cash usage, high cash generation. | **QUESTION MARKS**<br>• Strategy: Analyze or Divest.<br>• Profile: High cash usage, low cash generation. |
| **Low Market Growth** | **CASH COWS**<br>• Strategy: Milk to fund bets.<br>• Profile: Low cash usage, high cash generation. | **DOGS**<br>• Strategy: Divest or Harvest.<br>• Profile: Low cash usage, low cash generation. |

## Quadrant Strategies

### 1. Stars (High Growth, High Share)
* **Characteristics:** Leaders in high-velocity industries. They generate significant revenues but consume massive amounts of cash to maintain dominance against competitors.
* **Action:** Fund continuously to protect market share; they are your future Cash Cows.

### 2. Cash Cows (Low Growth, High Share)
* **Characteristics:** Dominant players in mature markets. Capital expenditures are minimal because growth has plateaued, resulting in immense, steady free cash flow.
* **Action:** Harvest cash. Minimize new reinvestment and redistribute the capital surpluses to fund "Stars" and promising "Question Marks."

### 3. Question Marks (High Growth, Low Share)
* **Characteristics:** Sub-scale operations in rapidly expanding markets. They require heavy cash injections to scale up, with no guarantee of achieving market leadership.
* **Action:** Perform rigorous analysis. Either double down to transform them into Stars, or divest if competitive entry barriers are insurmountable.

### 4. Dogs (Low Growth, Low Share)
* **Characteristics:** Low market share in stagnant or declining industries. They rarely generate positive economic cash flows and often tie up vital executive attention.
* **Action:** Harvest, sell off, or systematically liquidate to free up internal corporate infrastructure.

## Key Case Interview Questions
* Is the client inadvertently starving its 'Stars' by reinvesting cash flow back into unprofitable, legacy 'Dogs'?
* What structural capabilities can turn this 'Question Mark' into a dominant 'Star' before the industry growth phase closes?
