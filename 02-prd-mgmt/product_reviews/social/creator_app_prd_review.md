# PRD Review: Creator-Led Social Video App

---

## Overview

A **creator-led social video platform** is a multi-sided network where creators produce short-form content and users consume, engage, and build identity through discovery and community interaction.

In 2026, the dominant shift is toward:
- short-form, algorithmic discovery feeds  
- creator-first monetization models  
- authenticity over production polish  
- community-driven engagement loops

## Pain Points

| Pain Point | User Impact | Product Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Low creator retention | Content supply drops | High |
| Feed fatigue | Reduced engagement | High |
| Poor moderation | Safety and trust loss | High |
| Weak monetization | Creator churn | High |
| Generic recommendations | Lower relevance | Medium |

---

## Risks and Trade-offs

| Risk | Tradeoff | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Stronger algorithmic ranking | Less transparency | Better engagement, weaker trust |
| More monetization tools | UI complexity | Higher creator retention, lower simplicity |
| Strong moderation | Reduced content velocity | Safer platform, slower growth |
| AI-generated content | Authenticity dilution | Scalable creation, trust concerns |
| Deep personalization | Filter bubble risk | Higher engagement, lower diversity |

## Summary Insight

This product succeeds only if it balances three forces:
- **Creator incentives (money + growth)**
- **User engagement (feed + discovery)**
- **Platform trust (moderation + authenticity)**

The winning model is not just “short video app” — it is a **creator economy + identity graph + AI-driven discovery system**.
