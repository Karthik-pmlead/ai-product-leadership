# PRD Review: Community Moderation

## Overview

A community moderation platform helps manage user-generated content, enforce policies, protect creators and members, and reduce harmful behavior. In 2026, moderation is increasingly shaped by regulation, AI-assisted enforcement, and higher expectations around safety and transparency [web:115][web:138][web:141].

| Area | Review |
|---|---|
| Product | Community moderation and trust & safety platform. |
| Core use case | Detect, review, and act on harmful or policy-breaking content. |
| Platform model | Safety and governance infrastructure. |
| Primary value | Safer communities, better trust, and scalable enforcement. |
| Review type | PRD review with policy and operations emphasis. |

## Visual and Experience

The product should feel operational and clear. Moderators need queues, policy context, escalation paths, and outcome tracking, while community managers need trends and risk visibility.

| Visual Dimension | Assessment |
|---|---|
| Navigation | Queue-based and policy-based. |
| Information density | High. |
| Trust cues | Enforcement history, policy tags, and review status. |
| Consistency | Important across reports, actions, and analytics. |
| UX priority | Speed, clarity, and policy accuracy. |

## Pain Points

| Pain Point | User Impact | Product Risk |
|---|---|---|
| False positives | Over-moderation frustration. | High. |
| False negatives | Harm remains visible. | High. |
| Queue overload | Slow response. | High. |
| Policy inconsistency | Trust loss. | High. |
| Weak appeals | Perceived unfairness. | Medium. |

## Recommendations

| Recommendation | Why It Matters | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Use AI to prioritize, not fully replace, review | Improves scale without losing judgment. | High. |
| Make policies explainable and searchable | Improves consistency. | High. |
| Add appeal and escalation flows | Builds fairness. | High. |
| Track moderation outcomes by policy type | Improves governance. | High. |
| Give creators extra protection tools | Reduces churn and abuse. | Medium. |

## Metrics

| Metric | Why It Matters |
|---|---|

| False positive / negative rate | Quality signal. |
| Appeal success rate | Fairness indicator. |
| Moderator throughput | Operational efficiency. |
| Policy violation recurrence rate | Long-term safety measure. |
| User trust score | Community health metric. |

## Risks and Tradeoffs

| Risk | Tradeoff | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Stronger enforcement | More user friction. | Better safety, possible creator backlash. |
| More automation | Less human nuance. | Faster scale, trust risk. |
| More transparency | More policy gaming. | Better trust, harder enforcement. |
| Broader moderation scope | Higher cost. | Better safety coverage, more operations load. |

## Interview Use

Frame moderation as a balance between safety and freedom. In interviews, talk about policy clarity, moderation queues, false positives/negatives, and how the product protects trust without blocking healthy community participation.
