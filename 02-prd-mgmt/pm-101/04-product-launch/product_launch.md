### Launch steps
Product launch process set of steps a team takes to get the product to market
1. Pre launch Preparation step - Objective (Growth, revenue), KPIs, target users, finalize product, Build test, , identify risks, GTM, prepare assets
2. Launch - Announce launch, Release product, Track (Bugs)
3. Post launch - Track/monitor metrics, collect feedback, iterate improve, scale growth 
   
- **Eg: App store launch**
- Team we need approvals from
    - Legal
    - Marketing
    - Engineering
    - Leadership
- Define metrics: Downloads, stability, usage, retention

- **Product: New food delivery app**
    - Pre-launch: Build app, test ordering flow, create ads
    - Launch: Release app + run promotions
    - Post-launch: Track orders, fix bugs, improve delivery time

**Identifying risk for product launch**
1. Technical risks: Server downtime, bugs, integration failures
2. Market risks: Low adoption, competitor launches
3. Operational risks: Insufficient support staff, logistics delays
4. Financial risks: Budget overruns, marketing spend ineffective
5. Legal/compliance risks: Privacy, regulatory issues

Eg: Lets assume we are very successful in the US market (English only) and we are ready to launch into other countries and languages. The European Union looks like a good option. What risks can you identify for launching in the EU market? 
Engineering: Talk to the Engineering team and confirm that our system can handle the extra pressure of the market expansion 
Testing: Test the UI for the new languages

#### Product pricing
Things to consider
- What value does user get from using the product
- What does it cost to produce the product?
- What do competitors charge?

1. Ad Supported: The product is free, but it generates revenue from advertisers.
2. Cost-based pricing: The cost to produce the product is the base price and a mark-up is added.
3. Dynamic pricing : Price changes reflect changes in supply or demand, e.g. surge pricing.
4. Freemium: The basic product is free but you can purchase additional features or content, e.g. Dropbox.
5. Price discrimination: Different users are charged a different price for the same product, e.g. US airline tickets.
6. Value-based pricing: Price is based on the value the user gets from the product.

### Launch patterns
1. Gradual rollout - Launch the product to small group of users
    - A beta launch is a controlled release of a product to a limited audience before the full public launch. Its main purpose is to test the product in real-world conditions, gather feedback, and fix issues before scaling
3. Full launch - For hardware product, once on shelf it has to work for any user who purchases it.

- After launch, monitor KPIs and user feedback
    - No issues -> release the product to more users.
    - If KPIs look problematic or issues appear in user feedback, halt the rollout or roll the feature back. Fix it and try again.

### A/B Testing
- We don't want to make changes to the product without knowing if the change is really a good one.

- Eg: Develop a solution to the user's problem with entering their credit card and set up an A/B test to determine if the solution will work We have feedback from the field that our users are struggling to enter their credit card information.
- We do not know if this is a trust issue or just difficulty of typing.

- Potential Solutions

- Auto complete feature
- Amazon Pay
- Credit card scanner (OCR)

- Our hypothesis is that the credit card scanner will make it easier for users to complete transactions so we will see more complete transactions in Group B
