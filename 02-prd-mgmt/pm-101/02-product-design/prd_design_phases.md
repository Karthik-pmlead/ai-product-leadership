## Phases

### Phase 1: Understand
 - Create a shared understanding of problem, user and and competition
    - **Techniques:**
        - Lightning talk - 20 min presentation on User research, business goals, technology
        - Interviews - User interviews (Deeper understanding of user needs and pain points)
        - Competitive analysis
        - How might we
            - Eg: **How migh we** make it easier for people to find items?
        - Rose, Bud, Thorn
            - (positives, opportunities, negative/problems/shortcomings)
        - Affinity mapping
            - Related ideas are categorized into themes.
            - Speed: Easy to signup 
            - Simplify: Reduce the # of steps users need to tak
            - Growth: Acquiring new users

### Phase 2: Define
 - Set goals and work backwards to get there
    - Techniques:
        - Success Metrics: Focus on what you want to achieve and to define success
        - HEART framework: Measure the quality of user experience
        - 3 Dimensions
        - Goal is something user is trying to do
        - Signal is change in user behavior
        - Metric is a way to quantify user behavior change
    - Eg:
    - Goal: Backup photos
    - Signal: Upload a photo
    - Metric: # of photos uploaded per user per day

### Future Press Release
 - **Why write a future press release** 
     - Get everyone on the team aligned on the outcomes you are trying to achieve
     - Difference between press release and review
         - Press release is written by you
         - Review is written by 3rd party

### Phase 3: Sketch
  - Ideate and explore solution
  - In this phase we want to create and explore lots of ideas.
  - Technique: Crazy 8s 8 ideas in 8 min
  - How to decide which one to choose? Voting
  - Eg: Autopilot

### Phase 4: Decide
   - Choose one idea and prototype and validate
   - Techniques:
      - Questioning assumption is a good way to make sure you don't get stuck in specific way of thinking
         - Eg: Blackberry. Q: Should phones have buttons lead to touchscreen
      - Decision Matrix allows you to visualize the trade off between different ideas
         - Usually ideas worth pursuing will have High value and High effort/Low effort
      - Thinking hats
         - A method allows you to make sure other perspectives are being represented and discussed when deciding which idea to move forward to prototyping.

### Phase 5: Prototype
  - Prototype is a fast and cheap way to test and validate your idea.
  - Storyboard maps out the problem the user is facing
  - and the journey they will go through with your product to solve it.
  - Depends on the situation, you can choose paper/pen, video, presentation or wireframing.

### Phase 6: Validate
   - Validation is one of the most important part of the design sprint process.
   - It allows you to test your ideas without fully building out the idea.
   - You get feedback about your product from target usrs
   - Steps to prepare before meeting
       - Who is your target user?
       - Split your target users: power vs casual users
   - Technical feasibilty:
       - Level of effort required to build your idea

### Next steps
 - Move to implementation
 - Iterate on specifics of the prototype
 - Abandon the idea

### Evangelize
 - Summary of design sprint is shared with stakeholders to create buy-in for the project.
