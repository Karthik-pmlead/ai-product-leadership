### Introduction to Design Sprint
### Design sprint
- **What:** Time constrained process to explore and reduce the risk of building new products or features
- **Where:** Can be applied for new product development or internal process improvement
- **How:** Bring a cross functional team to explore a specific probem that is time bound and prototype solution and test with real users using design thinking methods

- Benefits:
    - Exploration: 
    - Best case: Team gets clarity on what exactly to build
    - Worst case: Team fails and their solution isn't viable
- **When:**
    - Its good to use when you have big open questions.
    - Eg: New product, new features for existing product, solve a complex problem
- **When not to use:**
    - When you have a clear product direction then you don't need design sprint

### Design Thinking
- Human centered iterative approach to solve problems
    - By deeply understanding users and needs
    - redefining problems, creative innovative solution and testing with real users
- Why involve the team?
    - Design sprint is highly collaborative
    - so including key team members is great strategy
    - to create buy-in
    - and sense of ownership

### Understanding the Design spring through an example

- Eg: Imagine your business is a grocery store chain concerned about increased competition from online shopping and delivery services
    - Design sprint is a tool to quickly explore new ideas and validate them.

#### Phases of Design Sprint
  
- **Phase 1: Understand**
    - Create a shared understanding of problem, user and and competition
      
- **Phase 2: Define**
    - Set goals and work backwards to get there
      
- **Phase 3: Sketch**
    - Ideate and explore solution

- **Phase 4: Decide**
    - Choose one idea and prototype and validate

- **Phase 5: Prototype**
    - Build prototype

- **Phase 6: Validate**
    - Check if the idea is solving real user need

### Outcome of Design Sprint
1. Working prototype
2. Insights and validation
3. List of other ideas

### Who should be part of design sprint
- No hard and fast rule.
- Depends on the problem we are trying to solve
- New product: Team members from Product, Design, Engineering, Research, Marketing, Leadership
- Optimize current product: Product, Design, Engineering, Data Science, Research

### Planning a design sprint
  - Sprint structure
  - Methodologies that will be used
  - Create a challenge statement to help understand the purpose of the design sprint
  - Eg: Reduce the # of abandoned carts by our online shoppers in the next month
