## PM Intro

### What is Product Management?
 - At its core, its about identifying and defining problems.
 - What are we build?
     - What the product does and people want it.
 - Who are we building it for?
     - Customer segment. Kids vs Scientific calculator
 - Why are we solving this problem?
 - What is the impact?
     - Business value
     - User value
 - When are we building it?
     - Roadmap
  
### Why is Product management important?
 - You need someone to make sure right problems are solved.
 - Need someone to focus on the user and the problem.
 - Orchestrate - Get team moving in same direction
 - Identify and define problems - Solve right problems
 - Solving for real users

### Role of PM
 - Solving the right problem
 - PM shepherd and orchestrate their product from idea all the way through launch and partner with wide variety of cross functional teams
 - Integrate various aspects of business into strategically focused whole, maximizing the value of product by understanding market needs.
 - Since they have dedicated attention to product line it results in better information about customers and competition.

    - PM is at intersection of Design, Business and Technology
        - Design: User needs, Motivations, Advocacy
        - Business: Goals, Align product to goals
        - Technology: Understand complexity, Risks and tradeoffs

#### Skills of PM
 - **Active Listening**
    - 3 Step Technique: Listen, Reflect, Respond
 
 - **Cultivate resilience**
    - Adversity (accept setbacks) , Belief (Maintain confidence in your abilities), Coping  (Develop mechanism to navigate difficult situations)
 
 - **Conflict resolution**
    - CALM (Clarify, Address emotions, Listen, Manage solutions)

 - **Time management**
    - Eisenhower Model
    - Important vs Urgent
    - Important and Urgent - Do it immediately
    - Important and Not Urgent - Decide
    - Not Important and Urgent - Delegate immediately
    - Not Important and Not Urgent - Later
    
 - **Negotiation**
    - Reach agreement when there is conflict
    - Competitive (You win, they Lose)
    - Collaborative (You win, they win)
    - Compromise (You win some, they win some)
    - Avoid (You Lose, they Lose)
    - Accomodate (You Lose, they win)
            
 - **Decision Making**
    - Speed reversibility matrix
    - Low risk  & Irreversible - Gradual rollout
    - High risk & Irreversible - Analyze before making decisions
    - Low risk  & Reversible - Move quickly
    - High risk & Reversible - Experiment: Experiment, Analyze and A/B test
  
### Master Time Management
 - 1-3-5 Rule
 - 1 big task to accomplish
 - 3 medium tasks
 - 5 small tasks

### New PM
1. Company
   - Learn about the company
   - How they make money
   - short term goals and objectives
   - current projects
2. Teams
    - Learn who does what
    - How things get done
    - Team Dynamics
3. Product Experience
    - Test the product
    - Reviews, feedback
    - Check out the Project dashboard
4. Process
    - What process exist and how things get done
    - Approval process
    - Listen to customer calls shadow support

# 90-Day Plan for New Product Manager

| Phase        | Timeframe  | Focus                  | Key Actions                                                                 | Outputs                          |
|--------------|------------|------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Learn        | Days 0–30  | Understand & Listen    | - 1:1s with team (Eng, Design, Sales, Support) <br> - Use the product <br> - Review docs (PRDs, roadmap) <br> - Analyze metrics | - Product understanding doc <br> - Stakeholder map <br> - List of problems |
| Focus        | Days 30–60 | Prioritize & Align     | - Identify top problems <br> - Form product POV <br> - Align stakeholders <br> - Draft roadmap | - Clear priorities <br> - Roadmap <br> - Initial PRDs |
| Deliver      | Days 60–90 | Execute & Show Impact  | - Ship key feature/improvement <br> - Track metrics <br> - Improve team processes <br> - Communicate updates | - Shipped feature <br> - Measured impact <br> - Stakeholder updates |

## Guiding Principles

| Principle              | Description                                     |
|-----------------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| Trust > Speed         | Build credibility before making big changes      |
| Listen First          | Learn deeply before forming strong opinions      |
| Focus on Impact       | Prioritize a few high-value problems             |
| Communicate Clearly   | Keep stakeholders aligned and informed           |

## Key Questions by Phase

| Phase   | Questions to Answer                          |
|---------|----------------------------------------------|
| 0–30    | Why does this product exist?                 |
| 30–60   | What should we focus on next?                |
| 60–90   | Are we delivering real impact?               |
