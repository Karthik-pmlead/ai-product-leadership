# Decision Memo

## Overview

This document presents a recommendation, the evidence behind it, and the decision being requested. It is meant to help leaders make a clear, informed choice.

## Decision requested

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Decision | Approve onboarding + lifecycle nudges initiative |
| Owner | Product Manager |
| Date needed | 2026-05-27 |
| Decision maker | Head of Product |

## Context

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| Background | The product has decent acquisition but weak activation and retention. |
| Why this decision is needed now | The team must choose between doing nothing, onboarding only, or a broader retention push. |
| What triggered it | A recent analysis showed drop-off in the first-week experience. |

## Options

| Option | Pros | Cons | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Do nothing | No cost. | Retention remains weak. | No |
| Improve onboarding only | Quickest implementation. | May not solve re-engagement. | Maybe |
| Improve onboarding + nudges | Addresses both activation and retention. | Slightly more complex. | **Yes** |

## Evidence

| Evidence type | Details |
|---|---|
| User data | 68% of users drop before completing setup. |
| Market data | Competitors use guided onboarding and lifecycle email flows. |
| Financial data | Improving retention should improve CAC payback. |
| Operational impact | Support tickets about setup are increasing. |

## Recommendation

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| Recommended choice | Approve the combined onboarding and lifecycle initiative. |
| Why this option | It has the best balance of impact and effort. |
| Tradeoffs accepted | More coordination across product, marketing, and engineering. |

## Risks and mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Users may not respond to nudges | Use behavioral segmentation and frequency caps. |
| Engineering work may slip | Limit scope to one release cycle. |

## Implementation notes

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| Next steps | Finalize scope, build UX, configure emails, instrument metrics. |
| Dependencies | Product analytics, CRM tooling, engineering bandwidth. |
| Timing | 6-week implementation. |

## Notes

- Make the recommendation explicit.
- Use facts and assumptions separately.
- Keep the memo focused on decision quality.
