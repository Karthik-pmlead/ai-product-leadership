# Product Strategy One-Pager

## Overview

This document captures product strategy on a single page. It is useful for leadership alignment and quick strategic communication.

## Winning aspiration

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| What are we trying to achieve | Become the easiest product in the category for users to reach first value. |
| Why this matters | Faster value delivery should improve retention and monetization. |

## How will we know we’ve won?

| Metric | Baseline | Target |
|---|---:|---:|
| Activation rate | 32% | 50% |
| 30-day retention | 18% | 28% |
| ARPU | $24 | $26.40 |

## Where we’ll play

| Segment | Why this segment | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Self-serve mid-market users | Highest volume and fastest path to value. | High |
| New signups from organic traffic | Largest source of top-of-funnel growth. | High |
| Inactive returning users | Good chance for re-engagement. | Medium |

## How we’ll win

| Bet | Why it matters | Expected outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Faster onboarding | Reduces friction before first value. | Higher activation |
| Guided setup | Helps users complete key setup steps. | Better retention |
| Lifecycle nudges | Re-engages users who stall or churn. | More repeat usage |

## Key capabilities and considerations

| Capability / consideration | Why needed |
|---|---|
| Event tracking | To measure onboarding and retention accurately. |
| Messaging automation | To send timely nudges and reminders. |
| UX simplification | To reduce steps before first value. |
| Frequency control | To avoid over-messaging users. |

## Product principles

| Principle | What it means in practice |
|---|---|
| Reduce time to value | Every step should move users closer to their first success. |
| Guide, don’t overwhelm | Show only the next relevant step. |
| Measure everything important | Instrument activation, retention, and engagement. |

## Notes

- Keep this short and strategic.
- Use it to align around the core choices.
- Tie it to measurable outcomes.
