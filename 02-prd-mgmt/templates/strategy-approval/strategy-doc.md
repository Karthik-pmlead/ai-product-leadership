## Problem or opportunity

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| Current situation | The product has strong sign-up traffic, but low activation and weak repeat usage. |
| Why this matters now | Growth spend is increasing while retention is flat. |
| User or business pain | New users do not reach value fast enough, and the team lacks a clear focus on retention. |
| Market context | Competitors are improving onboarding, personalization, and lifecycle messaging. |

## Goals

| Goal | Success measure |
|---|---|
| Improve activation | Increase first-week activation from 32% to 50%. |
| Improve retention | Improve 30-day retention from 18% to 28%. |
| Improve monetization | Increase ARPU by 10% without hurting conversion. |

## Strategic choices

| Question | Decision |
|---|---|
| Where will we play? | Focus on self-serve mid-market customers. |
| How will we win? | Faster onboarding, clearer value delivery, and lifecycle nudges. |
| What won’t we do? | We will not expand to enterprise workflows this quarter. |

## Key bets

| Bet | Why it matters | Expected impact | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reduce onboarding steps | Cuts time to value. | Higher activation. | Product |
| Add guided setup | Helps users complete first success action. | Higher retention. | Design / Eng |
| Trigger lifecycle emails | Re-engages dormant users. | Better repeat usage. | Marketing |
