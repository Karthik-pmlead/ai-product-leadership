# NPS

## Definition

NPS (Net Promoter Score) measures customer loyalty by asking how likely a customer is to recommend a product or service to others [web:218][web:220][web:224].

## Simple formula

| Formula | Meaning |
|---|---|
| % Promoters - % Detractors | Net Promoter Score [web:218]. |

## Scoring bands

| Score range | Label |
|---|---|
| 0 to 6 | Detractors |
| 7 to 8 | Passives |
| 9 to 10 | Promoters [web:218]. |

## Example

Assume:
- 100 responses.
- 60 promoters.
- 20 passives.
- 20 detractors.

Calculation:
- NPS = 60% - 20% = 40.

| Item | Value |
|---|---:|
| Promoters | 60% |
| Detractors | 20% |
| NPS | 40 |

## Why it matters

| Use case | Why NPS matters |
|---|---|
| Brand health | Tracks customer loyalty. |
| Product strategy | Shows overall satisfaction direction. |
| Support and service | Helps identify pain points. |
| Benchmarking | Useful for comparing over time. |

## Good rules of thumb

| Rule | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Use alongside behavioral metrics | NPS alone does not show usage or revenue. |
| Track by segment | Different cohorts may have different loyalty levels. |
| Track by journey stage | Onboarding, checkout, and support can differ. |
| Use sample size carefully | Small samples can be misleading. |

## Interview use

In interviews, define NPS as a loyalty metric and explain that it is best used with retention, satisfaction, and usage metrics rather than alone.
