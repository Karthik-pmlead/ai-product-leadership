# ARPU

## Definition

ARPU (Average Revenue Per User) measures the average revenue generated by each user or customer over a given time period [web:209][web:212][web:215].

## Simple formula

| Formula | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Total revenue ÷ Total users | Average revenue per user [web:209][web:212]. |

## Example

Assume:
- Monthly revenue = $50,000.
- Total users = 1,000.

Calculation:
- ARPU = $50,000 ÷ 1,000 = $50.

| Item | Value |
|---|---:|
| Monthly revenue | $50,000 |
| Total users | 1,000 |
| ARPU | $50 |

## Why it matters

| Use case | Why ARPU matters |
|---|---|
| Monetization | Shows how much value each user contributes. |
| Segmentation | Helps compare free, paid, and premium users. |
| Pricing | Helps evaluate pricing changes. |
| Growth | Helps understand whether user growth is high quality. |

## Good rules of thumb

| Rule | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Use a consistent time period | Monthly or annual ARPU should not be mixed. |
| Decide whether to include free users | Be explicit about total users vs paying users. |
| Compare by segment | Premium users may distort blended ARPU. |
| Pair with retention | High ARPU is less useful if retention is weak. |

## Related metric

| Metric | Difference |
|---|---|
| ARPPU | Average revenue per paying user only. |
| ARPU | Average revenue across all users, paying or not. |

## Interview use

In interviews, explain ARPU as a monetization metric and use it to discuss segment quality, pricing, and revenue efficiency.
