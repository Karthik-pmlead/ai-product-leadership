# MRR and ARR

## Definition

MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue) is the predictable recurring revenue a business earns each month. ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue) is the annualized version of recurring revenue [web:210][web:213][web:216].

## Simple formulas

| Metric | Formula | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| MRR | Sum of recurring monthly revenue from all customers | Use for monthly subscription businesses [web:210][web:213][web:216]. |
| ARR | MRR × 12 | Annualized recurring revenue [web:210][web:216]. |

## Example

Assume:
- 200 customers.
- Each customer pays $35/month.

Calculation:
- MRR = 200 × $35 = $7,000.
- ARR = $7,000 × 12 = $84,000.

| Item | Value |
|---|---:|
| Customers | 200 |
| Monthly price | $35 |
| MRR | $7,000 |
| ARR | $84,000 |

## Why it matters

| Use case | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| SaaS growth | Tracks recurring business health. |
| Forecasting | Helps predict future revenue. |
| Sales planning | Useful for quota and pipeline planning. |
| Investor reporting | Common subscription business metric. |

## Good rules of thumb

| Rule | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Separate recurring and one-time revenue | MRR should not include one-time charges. |
| Track new, expansion, and churn MRR | Helps explain growth quality. |
| Use ARR for annual planning | ARR is better for yearly view. |
| Use MRR for operational monitoring | MRR is more sensitive to changes. |

## MRR components

| Component | Meaning |
|---|---|
| New MRR | Revenue from new customers. |
| Expansion MRR | Additional revenue from existing customers. |
| Churn MRR | Lost revenue from cancellations. |
| Contraction MRR | Lost revenue from downgrades. |

## Interview use

In interviews, explain MRR as the heartbeat of subscription revenue and ARR as the annualized version. Then mention growth components like new, expansion, churn, and contraction MRR.
