# CSAT

## Definition

CSAT (Customer Satisfaction Score) measures how satisfied a customer is after a specific interaction, product experience, or support event [web:220][web:224].

## Simple formula

| Formula | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Number of satisfied responses ÷ Total responses × 100 | Customer Satisfaction Score. |

## Example

Assume:
- 200 survey responses.
- 150 users rate 4 or 5 out of 5.

Calculation:
- CSAT = 150 ÷ 200 × 100 = 75%.

| Item | Value |
|---|---:|
| Satisfied responses | 150 |
| Total responses | 200 |
| CSAT | 75% |

## Why it matters

| Use case | Why CSAT matters |
|---|---|
| Support | Measures resolution quality. |
| Checkout | Measures purchase experience. |
| Onboarding | Measures first-time user satisfaction. |
| Product improvements | Helps identify friction points. |

## Good rules of thumb

| Rule | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Measure after specific events | CSAT is best for moments, not overall sentiment. |
| Keep survey short | Higher response rates give better signal. |
| Segment by touchpoint | Support CSAT and checkout CSAT may differ. |
| Use with open-text feedback | Helps explain the score. |

## Interview use

In interviews, explain CSAT as a moment-based satisfaction metric and pair it with behavior metrics like conversion, retention, or resolution rate.
