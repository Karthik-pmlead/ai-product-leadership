# ROAS

## Definition

ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) measures how much revenue is generated for every unit of advertising spend [web:153][web:159].

## Simple formula

| Formula | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Revenue from ads ÷ Ad spend | Return on ad spend [web:153][web:159]. |

## Example

Assume:
- Ad spend = $5,000.
- Revenue from those ads = $20,000.

Calculation:
- ROAS = $20,000 ÷ $5,000 = 4.0x.

| Item | Value |
|---|---:|
| Ad spend | $5,000 |
| Revenue | $20,000 |
| ROAS | 4.0x |

## Why it matters

| Use case | Why ROAS matters |
|---|---|
| Paid marketing | Shows whether ads are profitable. |
| Channel comparison | Helps compare campaigns and platforms. |
| Budget allocation | Helps shift spend to better-performing channels. |
| Growth planning | Prevents inefficient scale. |

## Good rules of thumb

| Rule | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Measure ROAS by campaign | Avoid hiding poor ad groups in blended results. |
| Pair with CAC | ROAS alone may not show full unit economics. |
| Consider margin | Revenue is not the same as profit. |
| Track over time | Campaign efficiency changes quickly. |

## Interview use

In interviews, define ROAS as the revenue return on advertising spend and connect it to CAC, margin, and campaign-level decision making.
