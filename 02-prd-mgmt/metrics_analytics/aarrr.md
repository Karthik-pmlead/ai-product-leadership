# AARRR

## Definition

AARRR is a growth framework that stands for Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Revenue, and Referral [web:222][web:223].

## Meaning of each stage

| Stage | Meaning | Question to ask |
|---|---|---|
| Acquisition | Users discover or arrive at the product. | How do users find us? |
| Activation | Users reach first value. | Do users experience value quickly? |
| Retention | Users come back. | Do users return? |
| Revenue | Product generates money. | How do we monetize? |
| Referral | Users invite others. | Do users spread the product? |

## How to use it

| Step | What to do |
|---|---|
| 1 | Pick one key metric for each stage. |
| 2 | Find the biggest drop-off point. |
| 3 | Prioritize product changes that improve the weakest stage. |
| 4 | Re-measure after changes. |

## Example

For a consumer app:
- Acquisition = visits.
- Activation = account created.
- Retention = weekly active users.
- Revenue = paid conversions.
- Referral = invites sent.

## Why it matters

| Use case | Why AARRR matters |
|---|---|
| Growth diagnosis | Helps find funnel bottlenecks. |
| Product strategy | Connects product work to growth. |
| Cross-functional alignment | Works across marketing, product, and sales. |

## Good rules of thumb

| Rule | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Do not treat it as one metric | It is a framework, not a KPI. |
| Define metrics per product | Different products need different stage metrics. |
| Revisit often | Growth bottlenecks change over time. |

## Interview use

In interviews, use AARRR to structure growth analysis: acquisition, activation, retention, revenue, and referral.
