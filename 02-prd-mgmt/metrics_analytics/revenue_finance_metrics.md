# Revenue and Finance Metrics

## Overview

Revenue and finance metrics help teams understand growth, profitability, subscription health, and unit economics. These metrics are essential for SaaS, marketplaces, commerce, and any business that needs to balance growth with financial sustainability [web:253][web:256][web:259].

## Core metrics

| Metric | What it measures | Why it matters | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| MRR | Monthly recurring revenue. | Subscription revenue health. | SaaS core metric. |
| ARR | Annual recurring revenue. | Annualized subscription scale. | Useful for planning and forecasting. |
| ARPU | Average revenue per user. | Monetization efficiency. | Can be per user or per customer. |
| Gross Revenue | Total revenue before deductions. | Top-line performance. | Useful starting point. |
| Net Revenue | Revenue after refunds, discounts, and fees. | True earned revenue. | Better than gross in many cases. |
| Gross Margin | Revenue after direct costs. | Profitability. | Critical across business models. |
| Contribution Margin | Revenue after variable costs. | Unit economics. | Important for marketplaces and commerce. |
| Revenue Growth Rate | Period-over-period revenue growth. | Momentum indicator. | Useful across all models. |
| Net Revenue Retention | Existing-customer revenue after churn and expansion. | SaaS growth quality. | Strong retention and expansion signal [web:253][web:256][web:259]. |
| Gross Revenue Retention | Revenue retained before expansion. | Churn quality. | Focuses on lost revenue only [web:253][web:256]. |
| Expansion Revenue | Additional revenue from existing customers. | Growth from current accounts. | Important in SaaS and B2B. |
| Churn Revenue | Revenue lost from cancellations. | Retention risk. | Use with NRR and GRR. |
| AOV | Average order value. | Transaction value. | Strong commerce metric. |
| LTV / CLV | Customer lifetime value. | Profitability and acquisition planning. | Should be compared with CAC. |
| CAC | Customer acquisition cost. | Growth efficiency. | Important for unit economics. |
| CAC Payback Period | Time to recover acquisition cost. | Cash efficiency. | Helps assess growth sustainability. |
| Burn Rate | Cash spent per month or period. | Runway planning. | Important for startups. |
| Runway | Time before cash runs out. | Survival planning. | Based on burn and cash balance. |
| EBITDA | Operating profitability before interest, tax, depreciation, amortization. | Profitability view. | Common in mature businesses. |

## Use cases

| Use case | Most relevant metrics |
|---|---|
| SaaS growth | MRR, ARR, NRR, GRR, CAC, LTV |
| E-commerce | Gross Revenue, Net Revenue, AOV, Gross Margin |
| Marketplace | Gross Revenue, Take Rate, Contribution Margin |
| Startup planning | Burn Rate, Runway, CAC Payback Period |

## Practical tips

| Tip | Why it helps |
|---|---|
| Separate gross and net values | Gross can hide refunds and discounts. |
| Track retention and expansion together | Growth quality matters, not just new sales. |
| Use margins, not only revenue | Profitability can differ widely. |
| Review by customer segment | Different segments may have very different unit economics. |

## Interview use

In interviews, explain revenue and finance metrics as the bridge between product usage and business sustainability. Then connect recurring revenue, retention, margins, and acquisition cost to show whether growth is healthy.
