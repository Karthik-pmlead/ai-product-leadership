# CRM and Segmentation Metrics

## Overview

CRM and segmentation metrics help teams understand customer quality, lifecycle stage, account health, and campaign performance. They are especially useful for sales, customer success, lifecycle marketing, and retention planning [web:252][web:254][web:257].

## Core metrics

| Metric | What it measures | Why it matters | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| RFM Score | Recency, Frequency, and Monetary value. | Customer segmentation and prioritization. | Useful for lifecycle and CRM analysis. |
| Lead Score | Likelihood a lead will convert. | Sales prioritization. | Based on behavior, demographics, and engagement [web:252][web:257]. |
| MQL Rate | Percentage of leads classified as marketing qualified. | Funnel quality. | Bridge between marketing and sales. |
| SQL Rate | Percentage of leads classified as sales qualified. | Sales readiness. | Indicates lead intent and fit. |
| Opportunity Conversion Rate | Percentage of opportunities that close. | Revenue effectiveness. | Strong CRM metric. |
| Pipeline Value | Total value of open opportunities. | Sales forecasting. | Core CRM view. |
| Win Rate | Closed-won deals divided by opportunities. | Sales efficiency. | Useful by segment and lead source. |
| Average Deal Size | Average value per closed deal. | Revenue quality. | Important in enterprise sales. |
| Sales Cycle Length | Time taken to close a deal. | Sales speed. | Lower is better. |
| Expansion Revenue | Revenue from existing customers. | Growth from account management. | Critical in SaaS. |
| Customer Churn Rate | Percentage of customers lost over time. | Retention measure. | Important in subscriptions and CRM. |
| Renewal Rate | Percentage of customers renewing contracts. | Account health. | Strong for B2B and SaaS. |
| Adoption Rate | Share of users actively using the product. | Value realization. | Important for enterprise software. |
| Feature Adoption | Usage of specific features by users or accounts. | Product relevance. | Useful in CRM and SaaS. |
| Account Health Score | Combined engagement and risk signal. | Customer success prioritization. | Often internal and composite. |
| Customer Lifetime Value | Value of customer over time. | Profitability and prioritization. | Often paired with CAC. |
| Segmentation Accuracy | Quality of customer grouping. | Targeting effectiveness. | Useful in marketing CRM. |
| Campaign Response Rate | Responses to CRM campaigns. | Outreach effectiveness. | Important for lifecycle marketing. |
| NPS by Segment | Loyalty split across customer groups. | Identifies high-value cohorts. | Better than blended NPS alone. |

## Use cases

| Use case | Most relevant metrics |
|---|---|
| Sales prioritization | Lead Score, SQL Rate, Win Rate, Pipeline Value |
| Customer success | Account Health Score, Renewal Rate, Expansion Revenue, Churn Rate |
| Lifecycle marketing | RFM Score, Campaign Response Rate, NPS by Segment |
| Product adoption | Adoption Rate, Feature Adoption, Customer Churn Rate |

## Practical tips

| Tip | Why it helps |
|---|---|
| Segment before acting | Different cohorts need different messaging and support. |
| Use both lead and account metrics | Leads help acquisition; accounts help retention and expansion. |
| Track by source and cohort | Helps identify which channels bring the best customers. |
| Refresh scores regularly | Lead quality and account health change over time. |

## Interview use

In interviews, explain CRM and segmentation metrics as the signals used to prioritize prospects, retain customers, and grow accounts. Then show how lead scoring, RFM, and account health work together.
