# Viral Coefficient

## Overview

The Viral Coefficient measures how effectively existing users bring in new users.

It quantifies:
- virality
- referral efficiency
- network-driven growth

A product is considered truly viral when:
- each user generates more than one additional user on average.

# Interpretation

| Viral Coefficient | Meaning |
|---|---|
| < 1 | Growth eventually slows |
| = 1 | Stable viral growth |
| > 1 | Exponential viral growth |

# Advantages

- Measures viral efficiency
- Helps optimize referral systems
- Supports scalable growth

---

# Limitations

- Virality alone doesn't guarantee retention
- Artificial incentives may reduce quality
- Sustainable virality is difficult

---

# Best Practices

- Make sharing frictionless
- Embed collaboration naturally
- Deliver value before referral prompts
- Shorten viral cycle time
