# ICE Scoring Framework

## Overview

ICE Scoring is a lightweight prioritization framework used to evaluate ideas, features, experiments, or initiatives quickly.

ICE stands for:
- Impact
- Confidence
- Ease

It is commonly used by startups, growth teams, and product managers when rapid prioritization is needed.

Typical scoring scale:
- 1 = Low
- 10 = High

---

# Components

| Factor | Description |
|---|---|
| Impact | Expected business/customer impact |
| Confidence | Confidence in assumptions/data |
| Ease | Simplicity or implementation effort |

---

# Best Used When

- Rapid prioritization is needed
- Data is incomplete
- Growth experimentation
- Startup product roadmaps
- MVP planning

---

# Advantages

- Simple and fast
- Easy for cross-functional teams
- Encourages decision-making
- Works well in early-stage products

---

# Limitations

- Subjective scoring
- Less rigorous than RICE
- Can oversimplify complexity
- Different teams may score inconsistently

---

# Example

| Initiative | Impact | Confidence | Ease | Score |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Improve onboarding | 8 | 7 | 9 | 50.4 |
| Add dark mode | 4 | 8 | 8 | 25.6 |
| AI recommendations | 10 | 4 | 3 | 12 |
