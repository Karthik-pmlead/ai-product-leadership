# Opportunity Scoring

## Overview

Opportunity Scoring is a product discovery framework used to identify underserved customer needs.

Popularized by:
- Anthony Ulwick
- Outcome-Driven Innovation (ODI)

The framework compares:
- Importance of a customer outcome
- Customer satisfaction with current solutions

---

# Core Idea

High importance + low satisfaction = opportunity.

# Components

| Component | Description |
|---|---|
| Importance | How important the outcome is to users |
| Satisfaction | How satisfied users are currently |

---

# Best Used When

- Product discovery
- Identifying unmet needs
- Market differentiation
- Innovation planning
- Customer research

---

# Opportunity Types

| Scenario | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| High importance + low satisfaction | Major opportunity |
| High importance + high satisfaction | Competitive parity |
| Low importance + low satisfaction | Low-value area |

---

# Advantages

- Customer-centric prioritization
- Identifies unmet needs
- Supports innovation discovery
- Useful for product-market fit

---

# Limitations

- Requires strong customer research
- Survey quality matters
- Can oversimplify nuanced behavior

---

# Example

| Outcome | Importance | Satisfaction | Opportunity |
|---|---|---|---|
| Faster checkout | 9 | 4 | High |
| Better avatars | 4 | 7 | Low |
