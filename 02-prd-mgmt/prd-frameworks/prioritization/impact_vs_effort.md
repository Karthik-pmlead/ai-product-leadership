# Impact vs Effort Matrix

## Overview

Impact vs Effort is a prioritization framework used to evaluate initiatives based on:
- Business/customer impact
- Required implementation effort

It helps teams quickly identify high-value opportunities.

---

# Matrix

| | Low Effort | High Effort |
|---|---|---|
| High Impact | Quick Wins | Major Projects |
| Low Impact | Fill-ins | Avoid |

---

# Best Used When

- Sprint planning
- Roadmap discussions
- Backlog refinement
- Stakeholder alignment
- Team prioritization workshops

---

# Quadrants

## Quick Wins
High impact + low effort.

Prioritize first.

Examples:
- UX improvements
- Performance optimizations
- Copy updates

---

## Major Projects
High impact + high effort.

Require planning and investment.

Examples:
- Platform migration
- AI systems
- International expansion

---

## Fill-ins
Low impact + low effort.

Useful when resources are idle.

---

## Avoid
Low impact + high effort.

Usually not worth prioritizing.

---

# Advantages

- Extremely simple
- Easy visualization
- Fast decision-making
- Encourages ROI thinking

---

# Limitations

- Subjective scoring
- Doesn't capture urgency
- Ignores strategic alignment
- Can underestimate dependencies
