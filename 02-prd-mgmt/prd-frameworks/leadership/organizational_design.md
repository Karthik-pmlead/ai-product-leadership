# Organizational Design

## Overview

Organizational Design is the process of structuring:
- teams
- responsibilities
- reporting lines
- operating models
- workflows

to achieve business goals effectively.

Good organizational design improves:
- execution speed
- ownership clarity
- scalability
- collaboration

---

# Core Principle

Organizations should be designed around:
- value creation
- customer outcomes
- operational efficiency

not internal politics.

---

# Key Organizational Elements

| Element | Description |
|---|---|
| Structure | Team hierarchy & reporting |
| Roles | Responsibilities & ownership |
| Processes | Operational workflows |
| Communication | Information flow |
| Decision Rights | Governance & authority |

---

# Common Organizational Models

| Model | Description |
|---|---|
| Functional | Teams grouped by expertise |
| Cross-Functional | Teams aligned to products/outcomes |
| Matrix | Dual reporting structures |
| Platform Model | Shared infrastructure/services |
| Networked | Flexible decentralized teams |

---

# Example

## Functional Organization

```text
Engineering
Marketing
Sales
Design
```

Strength:
- specialization

Weakness:
- silos

---

## Product-Aligned Organization

```text
Growth Team
Payments Team
Search Team
```

Strength:
- customer ownership

Weakness:
- duplicated capabilities

---

# Organizational Design Principles

## Clear Ownership

Every major area should have:
- explicit accountability

---

## Minimize Coordination Overhead

Reduce:
- excessive dependencies
- communication bottlenecks

---

## Align Teams to Value Streams

Teams should map to:
- customer outcomes
- business capabilities

---

# Best Used When

- Scaling organizations
- Restructuring teams
- Platform transformation
- Product operating model design

---

# Advantages

- Improves execution efficiency
- Clarifies responsibilities
- Enables scalability

---

# Limitations

- Reorganizations can create instability
- Poorly designed structures create silos

---

# Best Practices

- Design around customer value
- Reduce cognitive load
- Revisit structure periodically
- Align incentives & ownership
