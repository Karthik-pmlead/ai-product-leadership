# Leadership Principles

## Overview

Leadership Principles are foundational values and behaviors that guide:
- decision-making
- team culture
- organizational expectations
- leadership conduct

Strong principles create:
- consistency
- trust
- accountability
- cultural alignment

---

# Purpose of Leadership Principles

Leadership principles help organizations:
- scale culture
- align decisions
- define expectations
- reinforce behaviors

---

# Common Leadership Themes

| Theme | Description |
|---|---|
| Ownership | Take responsibility for outcomes |
| Customer Obsession | Prioritize customer value |
| Bias for Action | Move quickly with informed judgment |
| Accountability | Commit to results |
| Continuous Learning | Improve constantly |
| Transparency | Communicate openly |
| Collaboration | Work across teams effectively |

---

# Example Leadership Principles

## Ownership

Leaders:
- act proactively
- solve problems directly
- avoid blame shifting

---

## Customer Focus

Decisions prioritize:
- long-term customer value

over:
- short-term internal convenience

---

## Bias for Action

Leaders balance:
- speed
- judgment
- iteration

without waiting for perfect certainty.

# Advantages

- Creates shared expectations
- Improves cultural consistency
- Strengthens accountability

---

# Limitations

- Empty principles without reinforcement lose credibility
- Overly generic principles lack usefulness

---

# Best Practices

- Keep principles concise
- Reinforce through leadership behavior
- Integrate into decision-making
- Evaluate behaviors consistently
