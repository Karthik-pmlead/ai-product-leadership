# Executive Communication

## Overview

Executive Communication is the practice of communicating:
- strategy
- priorities
- risks
- decisions
- outcomes

clearly and concisely to leadership stakeholders.

Strong executive communication enables:
- alignment
- faster decision-making
- organizational trust
- strategic clarity

---

# Core Principle

Executives care most about:
- business impact
- strategic alignment
- risks
- tradeoffs
- decisions required

not implementation detail.

---

# Key Characteristics

| Characteristic | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Clear | Easy to understand quickly |
| Concise | Minimal unnecessary detail |
| Structured | Logical flow |
| Outcome-Focused | Emphasizes business impact |
| Actionable | Highlights decisions & next steps |

---

# Communication Structure

A common executive update structure:

```text
Context
→ Problem / Opportunity
→ Impact
→ Recommendation
→ Risks
→ Decision Needed
```

---

# Executive Communication Types

| Type | Example |
|---|---|
| Strategic Updates | Quarterly business reviews |
| Operational Reviews | Incident updates |
| Decision Memos | Product investment proposals |
| Board Communication | Executive summaries |
| Crisis Communication | Outage escalation |

---

# Example

## Weak Communication

> “The engineering migration encountered several technical dependencies.”

## Better Communication

> “Migration delay may impact Q3 launch timeline by 2 weeks unless additional staffing is approved.”

---

# Best Used When

- Leadership reporting
- Stakeholder alignment
- Product strategy reviews
- Incident escalation
- Organizational change

---

# Common Mistakes

- Too much operational detail
- Lack of clear recommendation
- Missing risks/tradeoffs
- No explicit ask or decision

---

# Best Practices

- Lead with conclusions
- Use structured summaries
- Quantify impact
- Highlight tradeoffs clearly
- Tailor depth to audience
