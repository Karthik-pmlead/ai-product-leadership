# First Principles Thinking

## Overview

First Principles Thinking is a problem-solving approach that breaks complex problems into fundamental truths and rebuilds solutions from the ground up.

Instead of relying on:
- assumptions
- conventions
- analogies

It asks:
> “What do we know to be fundamentally true?”

Popularized by:
- Aristotle
- Elon Musk
- physics-based reasoning

---

# Core Idea

Break problems into:
- basic facts
- essential constraints
- irreducible truths

Then rebuild solutions logically.

---

# Process

1. Identify assumptions
2. Break problem into fundamentals
3. Challenge existing constraints
4. Reconstruct from basics
5. Explore new possibilities

---

# Example

## Traditional Thinking
Electric cars are expensive because batteries are expensive.

## First Principles Thinking
What are batteries fundamentally made of?
- lithium
- nickel
- cobalt
- aluminum

Can production methods reduce cost?

This reframes the problem.
