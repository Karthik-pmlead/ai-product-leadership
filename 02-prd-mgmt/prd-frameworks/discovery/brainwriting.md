# Brainwriting

## Overview

Brainwriting is a structured ideation technique where participants silently write ideas before sharing them.

Unlike brainstorming:
- it reduces loud-voice dominance
- encourages equal participation
- generates more diverse ideas

---

# Core Idea

People often think better independently before group discussion.

Brainwriting prioritizes:
- quantity
- inclusiveness
- creativity

---

# Common Format: 6-3-5 Method

| Metric | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 6 | Participants |
| 3 | Ideas each round |
| 5 | Minutes per round |

Process repeats multiple rounds.

---

# Process

1. Present problem statement
2. Participants silently write ideas
3. Pass ideas to next participant
4. Others expand/improve ideas
5. Repeat for several rounds
6. Discuss and prioritize

---

# Best Used When

- Team workshops
- Discovery sessions
- Remote collaboration
- Cross-functional ideation
- Inclusive brainstorming

---

# Advantages

- Prevents groupthink
- Encourages quieter participants
- Produces many ideas quickly
- Reduces hierarchy bias

---

# Limitations

- Less spontaneous discussion
- Requires facilitation
- Can feel structured/artificial

---

# Example

## Prompt
How might we improve onboarding retention?

Generated ideas:
- Interactive walkthroughs
- AI onboarding assistant
- Progress tracking
- Personalized setup

---

# Best Practices

- Keep prompts specific
- Encourage quantity over perfection
- Avoid early criticism
- Timebox sessions
