# Balanced Scorecard

## Overview

The Balanced Scorecard is a strategic management framework created by:
- Robert Kaplan
- David Norton

It helps organizations measure performance across multiple dimensions beyond just financial outcomes.

---

# The 4 Perspectives

| Perspective | Focus |
|---|---|
| Financial | Revenue, profitability |
| Customer | Satisfaction & loyalty |
| Internal Processes | Operational efficiency |
| Learning & Growth | Innovation & capability building |

---

# Core Idea

Organizations should balance:
- short-term financial outcomes
with
- long-term capability growth.

---

# Example Scorecard

| Perspective | Example Metric |
|---|---|
| Financial | Gross margin |
| Customer | NPS |
| Internal | Cycle time |
| Learning | Employee skill growth |

---

# Strategy Flow

```text
Learning & Growth
        ↓
Better Internal Processes
        ↓
Improved Customer Value
        ↓
Financial Outcomes
```

---

# Best Used When

- Strategic planning
- Enterprise performance management
- Organizational alignment
- Executive dashboards

---

# Advantages

- Holistic performance view
- Aligns strategy with metrics
- Encourages long-term thinking

---

# Limitations

- Can become overly metric-heavy
- Requires strong measurement discipline

---

# Best Practices

- Limit metric overload
- Align metrics to strategy
- Review regularly
- Balance leading + lagging indicators
