# Ansoff Matrix

## Overview

The Ansoff Matrix is a growth strategy framework that helps organizations identify expansion opportunities using:
- products
- markets

Created by:
- Igor Ansoff

---

# Four Growth Strategies

| | Existing Products | New Products |
|---|---|---|
| Existing Markets | Market Penetration | Product Development |
| New Markets | Market Development | Diversification |

---

# Strategies

## Market Penetration

Increase share within existing markets.

Examples:
- Discounts
- Marketing campaigns
- Retention improvements

---

## Product Development

Introduce new products to existing customers.

Examples:
- AI features
- Premium plans
- New integrations

---

## Market Development

Expand existing products into new markets.

Examples:
- International expansion
- New customer segments

---

## Diversification

Enter new markets with new products.

Highest risk strategy.

Examples:
- New business lines
- Adjacent industries

---

# Risk Levels

| Strategy | Risk |
|---|---|
| Market Penetration | Low |
| Product Development | Medium |
| Market Development | Medium |
| Diversification | High |

---

# Best Used When

- Growth planning

# Advantages

- Simple strategic visualization
- Encourages structured growth thinking
- Helps assess risk

---

# Limitations

- Oversimplifies execution complexity
- Doesn't account for competition deeply
