# Offensive vs Defensive Strategy

## Overview

Offensive and Defensive Strategies describe how organizations compete in the market.

- Offensive strategies focus on growth and market capture
- Defensive strategies focus on protecting position and minimizing risk

Organizations often use both depending on:
- market maturity
- competition
- business goals

---

# Offensive Strategy

## Goal
Gain market share and growth.

## Common Tactics

- Product innovation
- Aggressive pricing
- Market expansion
- Competitive disruption
- Viral growth

## Examples

- Launching AI-first products
- Expanding internationally
- Entering competitor territory

---

# Defensive Strategy

## Goal
Protect existing market position.

## Common Tactics

- Customer retention
- Ecosystem lock-in
- Operational efficiency
- Brand strengthening
- Cost optimization

## Examples

- Loyalty programs
- Long-term contracts
- Platform ecosystems

---

# Comparison

| Offensive | Defensive |
|---|---|
| Growth-focused | Protection-focused |
| Higher risk | Lower risk |
| Innovation-driven | Stability-driven |
| Market expansion | Market retention |

---

# Best Used When

| Situation | Recommended |
|---|---|
| Startup disruption | Offensive |
| Market leadership | Defensive |
| New category creation | Offensive |
| Economic uncertainty | Defensive |

---

# Advantages

- Encourages strategic clarity
- Helps resource allocation
- Useful for competitive planning

---

# Limitations

- Over-aggression can create instability
- Excessive defense slows innovation

---

# Example

## Offensive
A startup launches free AI tools to gain users rapidly.

## Defensive
An incumbent improves retention and ecosystem integration.
