# BCG Matrix

## Overview

The BCG Matrix is a portfolio management framework created by:
- Boston Consulting Group (BCG)

It categorizes products/business units based on:
- market growth
- market share

The goal is to optimize investment allocation.

---

# Matrix Structure

| | High Market Growth | Low Market Growth |
|---|---|---|
| High Market Share | Stars | Cash Cows |
| Low Market Share | Question Marks | Dogs |

---

# Categories

## Stars

High growth + high market share.

Require investment but may become future cash cows.

---

## Cash Cows

Low growth + high market share.

Generate stable profits.

---

## Question Marks

High growth + low market share.

Potential future stars or failures.

Require strategic decisions.

---

## Dogs

Low growth + low market share.

Usually low-return businesses.

---

# Visualization

```text
Market Growth ↑

      Stars        |   Question Marks
-------------------|-------------------
   Cash Cows       |       Dogs

```

---

# Best Used When

- Portfolio strategy
- Multi-product organizations
- Investment decisions
- Resource allocation

---

# Advantages

- Simple portfolio visualization
- Helps investment prioritization
- Useful for strategic reviews

---

# Limitations

- Oversimplifies markets
- Ignores synergies
- Market share alone isn't enough

---

# Example

| Product | Category |
|---|---|
| Core SaaS platform | Cash Cow |
| AI assistant | Question Mark |
| Legacy reporting tool | Dog |
