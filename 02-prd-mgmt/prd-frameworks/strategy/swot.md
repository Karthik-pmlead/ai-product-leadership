# SWOT Analysis

## Overview

SWOT Analysis is a strategic planning framework used to evaluate:
- Strengths
- Weaknesses
- Opportunities
- Threats

It helps organizations assess:
- internal capabilities
- external market conditions

---

# SWOT Matrix

| Internal Factors | External Factors |
|---|---|
| Strengths | Opportunities |
| Weaknesses | Threats |

---

# Components

## Strengths
Internal advantages.

Examples:
- Strong brand
- Loyal customers
- Proprietary technology

---

## Weaknesses
Internal limitations.

Examples:
- Technical debt
- Limited funding
- Poor UX

---

## Opportunities
External favorable conditions.

Examples:
- Emerging markets
- AI adoption trends
- Regulatory changes

---

## Threats
External risks.

Examples:
- New competitors
- Economic downturn
- Platform dependency

---

# Best Used When

- Strategic planning
- Market analysis
- Product strategy
- Competitive assessment
- Business reviews

---

# Advantages

- Simple and widely understood
- Encourages strategic thinking
- Balances internal/external analysis
- Useful across industries

---

# Limitations

- Can become overly broad
- Subjective analysis possible
- Does not prioritize actions
- Static snapshot in time

---

# Example

| Strengths | Weaknesses |
|---|---|
| Strong engineering team | Slow onboarding |

| Opportunities | Threats |
|---|---|
| AI market growth | New competitors |

---

# Best Practices

- Use data-backed insights
- Prioritize critical factors
- Convert findings into actions
- Revisit periodically
