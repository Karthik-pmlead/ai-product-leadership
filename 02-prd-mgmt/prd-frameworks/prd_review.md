# PRD Review Framework

## Overview

A PRD Review Framework ensures that a Product Requirements Document (PRD) is:
- clear
- actionable
- aligned
- testable
- execution-ready

The goal is to reduce:
- ambiguity
- misalignment
- delivery risk

before engineering execution begins.

---

# Core Review Areas

| Area | Key Question |
|---|---|
| Problem Clarity | Is the problem clearly defined? |
| User Value | Does it solve a real user need? |
| Scope | Is scope well-bounded? |
| Requirements | Are requirements unambiguous? |
| UX Flow | Is the experience coherent? |
| Metrics | Are success metrics defined? |
| Dependencies | Are risks/dependencies identified? |
| Feasibility | Is implementation realistic? |

---

# PRD Review Checklist

## Problem Definition

- Is the customer problem clear?
- Is supporting evidence included?
- Is business impact explained?

---

## User & Customer Clarity

- Are target users identified?
- Are JTBD/personas referenced?
- Is user pain quantified?

---

## Requirements Quality

- Are acceptance criteria clear?
- Are edge cases covered?
- Are assumptions documented?

---

## UX & Flow

- Are workflows documented?
- Are wireframes/mockups linked?
- Are error states defined?

---

## Metrics & Success

- Are success metrics measurable?
- Is instrumentation defined?
- Are guardrail metrics included?

---

## Technical Feasibility

- Are constraints identified?
- Are dependencies clear?
- Is rollout strategy defined?

---

# Example Review Questions

| Category | Review Question |
|---|---|
| Scope | Is MVP scope realistic? |

---

# Best Used When

- Feature planning
- Product reviews
- Cross-functional alignment
- Engineering handoff
- Product governance

---

# Advantages

- Improves PRD quality
- Reduces ambiguity
- Prevents downstream rework
- Aligns teams early

---

# Limitations

- Overly rigid reviews slow execution
- Excessive detail can reduce agility

---

# Best Practices

- Focus on clarity over verbosity
- Review collaboratively
- Use templates/checklists
- Keep PRDs outcome-oriented
