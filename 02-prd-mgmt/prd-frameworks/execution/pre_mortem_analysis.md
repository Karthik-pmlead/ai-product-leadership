# Pre-Mortem Analysis

## Overview

Pre-Mortem Analysis is a risk identification technique where teams imagine a future project failure and work backward to identify possible causes.

Popularized by:
- Gary Klein

Unlike post-mortems:
- pre-mortems happen before execution.

---

# Core Idea

Assume:
> “The project failed badly.”

Then ask:
> “What caused the failure?”

This encourages proactive risk discovery.

---

# Process

1. Imagine project failure
2. Individually list possible causes
3. Share risks collaboratively
4. Identify high-risk themes
5. Define mitigation plans

---

# Example

## Scenario
Product launch failed.

### Potential Causes
- Poor onboarding
- Scalability issues
- Weak positioning
- Lack of customer demand

### Mitigations
- Load testing
- Beta programs
- Messaging validation

---

# Best Used When

- Major launches
- Strategic initiatives
- High-risk projects
- Cross-functional planning

---

# Advantages

- Reduces optimism bias
- Encourages risk awareness
- Improves planning quality
- Surfaces hidden concerns

---

# Limitations

- Can become overly pessimistic
- Requires psychological safety
- Risks may still be unknown

---

# Best Practices

- Encourage open participation
- Focus on actionable risks
- Prioritize mitigation strategies
- Revisit periodically
