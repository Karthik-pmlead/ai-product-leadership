# Scrum

## Overview

Scrum is an Agile framework for delivering work iteratively in fixed-length cycles called:
- sprints

It helps teams:
- organize work
- collaborate effectively
- deliver value continuously

---

# Core Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Product Owner | Prioritizes backlog |
| Scrum Master | Facilitates Scrum process |
| Development Team | Delivers work |

---

# Scrum Artifacts

| Artifact | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Product Backlog | Prioritized work list |
| Sprint Backlog | Work for current sprint |
| Increment | Working deliverable |

---

# Scrum Events

| Event | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Sprint Planning | Define sprint goals |
| Daily Standup | Team synchronization |
| Sprint Review | Demonstrate outcomes |
| Retrospective | Process improvement |

---

# Sprint Flow

```text
Backlog
   ↓
Sprint Planning
   ↓
Sprint Execution
   ↓
Review
   ↓
Retrospective
   ↓
Next Sprint
```

---

# Best Used When

- Product development
- Cross-functional teams
- Iterative delivery
- Agile organizations

---

# Advantages

- Predictable delivery cadence
- Strong team alignment
- Continuous improvement
- Clear prioritization

---

# Limitations

- Excessive ceremony possible
- Poor backlog quality hurts delivery
- Sprint rigidity may reduce flexibility

---

# Best Practices

- Keep sprint goals clear
- Maintain healthy backlog refinement
- Focus on outcomes, not velocity alone
- Conduct actionable retrospectives
