# Cynefin Framework

## Overview

The Cynefin Framework is a decision-making model created by:
- Dave Snowden

It helps leaders determine how to respond based on the complexity of situations.

Cynefin classifies problems into different domains:
- simple
- complicated
- complex
- chaotic
- disorder

---

# Cynefin Domains

| Domain | Nature | Recommended Approach |
|---|---|---|
| Clear (Simple) | Predictable | Sense → Categorize → Respond |
| Complicated | Expert analysis required | Sense → Analyze → Respond |
| Complex | Unpredictable | Probe → Sense → Respond |
| Chaotic | Crisis state | Act → Sense → Respond |
| Disorder | Unclear domain | Gather clarity |

---

# Domain Breakdown

## Clear / Simple

Characteristics:
- stable
- repeatable
- known best practices

Examples:
- payroll processing
- standard operations

---

## Complicated

Characteristics:
- requires expertise
- multiple correct answers possible

Examples:
- architecture design
- financial forecasting

---

## Complex

Characteristics:
- emergent outcomes
- uncertainty
- experimentation required

Examples:
- startup innovation
- organizational culture change

---

## Chaotic

Characteristics:
- immediate instability
- crisis response needed

Examples:
- major outages
- cybersecurity incidents

# Limitations

- Abstract for beginners
- Domain classification may be subjective

# Example

## Product Launch

| Situation | Domain |
|---|---|
| Stable onboarding optimization | Complicated |
| New AI category creation | Complex |

# Best Practices

- Match approach to complexity
- Avoid applying rigid processes everywhere
- Use experimentation in uncertainty
- Stabilize chaos quickly
