# RAPID Decision Framework

## Overview

RAPID is a decision-making framework developed by:
- Bain & Company

It clarifies decision roles to improve:
- speed
- accountability
- alignment

Unlike RACI, RAPID focuses specifically on:
- decision governance

---

# RAPID Meaning

| Letter | Meaning | Description |
|---|---|---|
| R | Recommend | Proposes solution/recommendation |
| A | Agree | Must approve before decision |
| P | Perform | Executes the decision |
| I | Input | Provides expertise/input |
| D | Decide | Final decision-maker |

---

# Core Idea

Decision-making becomes faster when:
- roles are explicitly defined.

---

# Example

## Product Pricing Decision

| Role | Team |
|---|---|
| Recommend | Product Manager |
| Agree | Finance |
| Input | Sales & Marketing |
| Decide | VP Product |
| Perform | Operations |

---

# Best Used When

- Strategic decisions
- Enterprise governance
- Complex stakeholder environments
- High-impact product decisions

---

# Advantages

- Clarifies decision ownership
- Reduces approval bottlenecks
- Improves accountability
- Speeds organizational alignment

---

# Limitations

- Over-structuring slows small teams
- Requires organizational discipline
- Role confusion weakens effectiveness

---

# RACI vs RAPID

| RACI | RAPID |
|---|---|
| Task ownership | Decision ownership |
| Execution-focused | Governance-focused |
| Operational clarity | Decision clarity |

---

# Best Practices

- Keep decision ownership explicit
- Minimize approval layers
- Document major decisions
- Use for high-impact decisions only
