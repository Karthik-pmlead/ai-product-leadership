## RFM

📌**What is RFM?**

RFM is a customer segmentation framework used to analyze user behavior based on purchase history.

It stands for:

R → Recency: How recently did the user purchase?
F → Frequency: How often do they purchase?
M → Monetary: How much do they spend?

👉 It helps identify high-value vs low-value customers

⏰ **When to Use RFM**

Use RFM when:

- You want to segment customers
- You are running growth or retention analysis
- You want to identify loyal vs churn-risk users
- You are building CRM / marketing campaigns
- You want to improve customer lifetime value (LTV)

👉 Best for: e-commerce, marketplaces, subscription apps

📍 **Where to Use RFM**
📈 Customer analytics dashboards
📧 Email / CRM targeting
🎯 Retention & re-engagement campaigns
💰 Revenue optimization strategy
🧠 User segmentation for growth teams

⚙️ **How RFM Works**

You score users on each dimension (usually 1–5 scale):

- Metric	Meaning
- Recency	How recently user purchased
- Frequency	How often user purchases
- Monetary	Total money spent

Then combine scores → e.g. 555 = best customers

📊 **RFM Example (Food Delivery App)**
# RFM Customer Segmentation – Food Delivery App

| Customer Type        | Recency | Frequency | Monetary | Interpretation |
|---------------------|----------|-----------|----------|----------------|
| Champions           | 5        | 5         | 5        | Loyal high-value users |
| Loyal Customers     | 4–5      | 4–5       | 3–5      | Regular repeat users |
| Potential Loyalists | 3–5      | 2–3       | 2–3      | Growing engagement |
| At Risk             | 1–2      | 2–4       | 2–5      | Haven’t ordered recently |
| Hibernating         | 1        | 1         | 1–2      | Almost inactive users |

### Interpretation
| Segment         | Strategy                   |
| --------------- | -------------------------- |
| Champions       | Reward, retain, VIP perks  |
| Loyal users     | Upsell, increase frequency |
| Potential users | Nudge with offers          |
| At-risk         | Win-back campaigns         |
| Hibernating     | Re-engagement or ignore    |

RFM is powerful because it helps you:

Stop treating all users the same
Focus on high-value customers
Improve retention and revenue
Drive targeted personalization
