# LTV : CAC

## Overview

LTV : CAC is a core business metric used to evaluate the sustainability and efficiency of customer acquisition.

It compares:
- Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)
vs
- Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

---

# Definitions

## LTV (Lifetime Value)

The total revenue expected from a customer during their relationship with the business.

---

## CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)

The total cost required to acquire a new customer.

Includes:
- marketing
- sales
- advertising
- onboarding costs

# Best Used When

- SaaS businesses
- Subscription products
- Growth planning
- Unit economics analysis

---

# Advantages

- Measures business sustainability
- Supports growth investment decisions
- Helps optimize acquisition efficiency

---

# Limitations

- Requires accurate retention estimates
- Simplified models may mislead
- Delayed revenue complicates analysis

---

# Example

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| LTV | $900 |
| CAC | $300 |
| Ratio | 3:1 |

# Best Practices

- Monitor by acquisition channel
- Segment customer cohorts
- Include churn impact
- Track payback period
