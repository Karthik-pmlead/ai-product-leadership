# AARRR Framework (Pirate Metrics)

## Overview

AARRR is a growth framework used to measure and optimize the customer lifecycle.

Created by:
- Dave McClure

The framework focuses on five critical stages of growth.

---

# The 5 Stages

| Stage | Goal |
|---|---|
| Acquisition | How users discover you |
| Activation | First successful experience |
| Retention | Users return consistently |
| Revenue | Monetization |
| Referral | Users invite others |

---

# Visualization

```text
Acquisition → Activation → Retention → Revenue → Referral
```

---

# Stage Breakdown

## Acquisition

How users find the product.

Examples:
- SEO
- Ads
- Social media
- Product-led growth

### Common Metrics
- Traffic
- CAC
- Signup rate

---

## Activation

Users experience initial value.

Examples:
- Completing onboarding
- Sending first message
- First successful transaction

### Common Metrics
- Activation rate
- Time-to-value

---

## Retention

Users continue using the product.

### Common Metrics
- DAU/MAU
- Churn rate
- Retention cohorts

---

## Revenue

Users generate business value.

### Common Metrics
- ARPU
- MRR
- Conversion rate
- LTV

---

## Referral

Users bring additional users.

### Common Metrics
- Viral coefficient
- Referral rate
- Invite conversion

---

# Best Used When

- SaaS growth
- Product-led growth
- Startup analytics
- Funnel optimization

---

# Advantages

- Simple lifecycle model
- Strong growth focus
- Easy cross-team alignment
- Actionable metrics

---

# Limitations

- Oversimplifies complex journeys
- B2B journeys may differ
- Doesn't deeply capture customer experience

---
