# Growth Accounting

## Overview

Growth Accounting is a framework used to understand:
- where user growth comes from
- how users are retained or lost
- whether growth is sustainable

Instead of only tracking total users, growth accounting breaks growth into:
- new users
- retained users
- resurrected users
- churned users

---

# Core User Categories

| Category | Meaning |
|---|---|
| New Users | First-time users |
| Retained Users | Existing returning users |
| Resurrected Users | Previously inactive users who returned |
| Churned Users | Users who stopped using product |

# Why It Matters

Traditional growth metrics can hide:
- poor retention
- heavy churn
- unsustainable acquisition dependence

Growth accounting reveals:
- true product health

---

# Key Questions Answered

- Is growth acquisition-driven or retention-driven?
- Are users returning naturally?
- Is churn offsetting acquisition?
- Are dormant users reactivating?

---

# Best Used When

- Product analytics
- Growth analysis
- Retention optimization
- Product-market fit evaluation

---

# Advantages

- Separates growth sources clearly
- Highlights retention quality
- Improves strategic decision-making

---

# Limitations

- Requires accurate cohort tracking
- Definitions vary across organizations

---

# Best Practices

- Analyze by cohort
- Segment by acquisition channel
- Track resurrection behavior
- Combine with retention curves
