# Voice of Customer (VoC)

## Overview

Voice of Customer (VoC) is the process of collecting, analyzing, and understanding customer feedback.

The goal is to understand:
- customer expectations
- frustrations
- motivations
- unmet needs

VoC programs help organizations become customer-centric.

---

# Common VoC Sources

| Source | Example |
|---|---|
| Surveys | NPS, CSAT |
| Interviews | User interviews |
| Support Tickets | Complaints/issues |
| Reviews | App store feedback |
| Social Media | Public sentiment |
| Analytics | Behavioral signals |

---

# Common Metrics

| Metric | Purpose |
|---|---|
| NPS | Loyalty measurement |
| CSAT | Satisfaction score |
| CES | Ease-of-use measurement |

---

# Best Used When

- Product discovery
- UX improvements
- Customer satisfaction tracking
- Churn analysis
- Market research

---

# Advantages

- Customer-centric insights
- Improves retention
- Helps identify unmet needs
- Supports prioritization

---

# Limitations

- Feedback can be biased
- Loud users may dominate
- Requires proper synthesis

---

# Best Practices

- Combine qualitative + quantitative data
- Close the feedback loop
- Segment customer feedback
- Look for patterns, not anecdotes

---

# Example

A SaaS company notices repeated complaints about:
- onboarding complexity
- unclear billing

This informs roadmap prioritization.
