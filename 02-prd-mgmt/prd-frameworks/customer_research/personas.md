# Personas

## Overview

Personas are fictional yet research-based representations of target users.

They help teams:
- understand customer needs
- align product decisions
- improve UX
- prioritize features effectively

Personas summarize:
- goals
- motivations
- behaviors
- frustrations
- contexts

---

# Purpose

Personas help teams answer:

- Who are we building for?
- What problems do users face?
- What motivates users?
- What outcomes matter most?

---

# Persona Structure

| Section | Description |
|---|---|
| Name | Fictional identity |
| Demographics | Age, role, background |
| Goals | Desired outcomes |
| Pain Points | Problems/frustrations |
| Behaviors | Usage patterns |
| Motivations | What drives decisions |
| Preferred Channels | Mobile, web, email, etc. |

---

# Example Persona

## Sarah — Busy Product Manager

| Attribute | Details |
|---|---|
| Role | Senior Product Manager |
| Goals | Ship faster with alignment |
| Pain Points | Too many meetings |
| Behavior | Mobile-first productivity tools |
| Motivation | Efficiency & visibility |

# Best Used When

- Product discovery
- UX design
- Marketing alignment
- Feature prioritization
- Journey mapping

---

# Advantages

- Creates user empathy
- Aligns teams around users
- Improves product decisions
- Simplifies communication

---

# Limitations

- Can become fictional stereotypes
- Requires real research
- Must evolve over time

---

# Best Practices

- Base personas on actual research
- Avoid excessive demographic focus
- Prioritize behaviors and goals
- Keep personas actionable
