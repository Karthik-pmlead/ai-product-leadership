# North Star Metric (NSM)

## Overview

A North Star Metric (NSM) is the single metric that best captures the core value delivered to customers.

It aligns teams around:
- customer value
- long-term growth
- strategic focus

The metric should reflect:
- sustainable business success
- customer success simultaneously

---

# Core Idea

Growth happens when customers repeatedly receive value.

The North Star Metric measures:
> delivered customer value at scale.

---

# Characteristics of a Good NSM

| Characteristic | Description |
|---|---|
| Customer-centric | Reflects user value |
| Leading indicator | Predicts long-term growth |
| Actionable | Teams can influence it |
| Simple | Easy to understand |

---

# Examples

| Company Type | North Star Metric |
|---|---|
| Streaming Platform | Hours watched |
| Marketplace | Successful transactions |
| SaaS Product | Weekly active teams |
| Social Platform | Daily engaged users |

---

# Best Used When

- Product strategy alignment
- Growth planning
- Cross-functional coordination
- Executive reporting

---

# Advantages

- Creates strategic focus
- Aligns teams
- Encourages customer-centric thinking
- Simplifies prioritization

---

# Limitations

- Oversimplification risk
- One metric cannot capture everything
- Poor NSM selection creates misalignment
