# Flywheel Model

## Overview

The Flywheel Model describes a self-reinforcing growth system where momentum compounds over time.

Unlike linear funnels:
- outputs become future inputs

Popularized by:
- Amazon

---

# Core Idea

Better experiences create:
- more customers
- more engagement
- stronger network effects
- lower acquisition costs

This accelerates growth.

---

# Example Flywheel

```text
Better Product
      ↓
More Customers
      ↓
More Data & Feedback
      ↓
Improved Experience
      ↓
Better Product
```

---

# Characteristics

| Trait | Description |
|---|---|
| Compounding | Growth accelerates |
| Self-reinforcing | Outputs feed inputs |
| Long-term | Momentum builds gradually |

---

# Best Used When

- Platform businesses
- SaaS growth
- Marketplaces
- Product-led growth systems

---

# Advantages

- Encourages systems thinking
- Supports sustainable growth
- Focuses on customer value

---

# Limitations

- Slow initial momentum
- Hard to build initially
- Requires operational alignment

---

# Example

## Amazon Flywheel

- Lower prices
- More customers
- More sellers
- Larger selection
- Better experience

Which further lowers prices.
