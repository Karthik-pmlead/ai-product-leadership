# OKRs (Objectives and Key Results)

## Overview

OKRs are a goal-setting framework used to align teams around measurable outcomes.

Popularized by:
- Intel
- Google

OKRs consist of:
- Objectives
- Key Results

---

# Structure

## Objective
Qualitative goal.

Answers:
> What do we want to achieve?

---

## Key Results
Quantitative measurable outcomes.

Answers:
> How will we measure success?

---

# Example

## Objective
Improve onboarding experience.

### Key Results
- Increase activation rate from 45% → 65%
- Reduce onboarding drop-off by 30%
- Improve onboarding CSAT to 4.5/5

---

# Characteristics of Good OKRs

| Objective | Key Result |
|---|---|
| Inspirational | Measurable |
| Clear | Time-bound |
| Outcome-oriented | Quantifiable |

---

# Best Used When

- Organizational alignment
- Quarterly planning
- Product strategy execution
- Growth tracking

---

# Advantages

- Creates focus
- Encourages measurable outcomes
- Aligns teams
- Improves accountability

---

# Limitations

- Poorly defined OKRs reduce value
- Too many OKRs create confusion
- Can encourage metric gaming

---

# Best Practices

- Focus on outcomes, not tasks
- Limit number of OKRs
- Review regularly
- Align with strategy
