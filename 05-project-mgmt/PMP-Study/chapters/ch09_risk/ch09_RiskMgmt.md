## Project Risk Mgmt
 - aims to identify and manage risks that are not addressed by the other project management processes
   - Individual project risk: Is an uncertain event or condition that, if it occurs, has
a positive or negative impact on one or more parts of the project.
   - Overall project risk: The risk exposure of the project as a whole. It’s made up
of the sum of individual project risks plus other sources of uncertainty.

### 2. Identify Risk
 - process of identifying individual project risks as well as 2)sources of overall project risk, & 3)documenting their characteristics
 - Key Benefit- Documentation of existing individual project risks and the sources of overall project risk.

**ITTO (Input, Tools & Techniques, Output)**
| Inputs                                      | Tools & Techniques                     | Outputs               |
|--------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|-----------------------|
| 1. Project Charter                         | 1. SWOT, Doc analysis                   | 1. Risk Register |
| 2. Project Plan                            | 2. Prompt lsits                      | 2. Risk Report |
| 3. EEFs (Enterprise Environmental Factors) | 3. Meetings               |                       |

#### Tools & Techniques
 - Checklist - Developed on basis of historical information
 - SWOT analysis - Examin the project SWOT perspective
 - Doc Analysis - Ambiguities in project doc
 - Prompt list - List of risk categories (PESTEL, TECOP, VUCA)

#### Key Output:  
1. Risk Register
  - List of identified risks
  - Potential risk owners
  - Potential risk responses

2. Risk Report
  - sources of overall project risk, together with summary information on identified individual project risks
  - content of the risk report may include
    - Sources of overall project risk
    - Summary information on identified individual project risk

### 3. Perform Qualitative Risk
  - process of prioritizing individual project risks for further analysis or action by assessing their probability of occurrence and impact as well as other
characteristics

 - Key Benefit- focuses efforts on high-priority risks

**ITTO (Input, Tools & Techniques, Output)**
| Inputs                                      | Tools & Techniques                     | Outputs               |
|--------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|-----------------------|
| 1. Risk plan                         | 1. Risk quality assesment, risk prob matrix                   | 1. Project doc updates |
| 2. Risk Register, stakeholder register                            | 2. Risk categorize, Impact matrix                      | 2. Assumption log, risk register, risk report |

1. Risk Data Quality Assessment
   - Evaluates the degree to which the data about individual project risks is accurate and reliable as a basis for qualitative risk analysis (completeness, objectivity, relevancy, and timeliness.)

2. Risk Probability and Impact Assessment
   - Risk probability assessment considers the likelihood that a specific risk will occur
   - Risks with low probability and impact may be included within the risk register as part of a watch list for future monitoring

3. Assessment of other risk parameters
   - Urgency, Proximity, Dormancy, Manageability, Controllability, Detectability

4. Risk Categorization
   - Source of risk, root causes
  
### Tools & Techniques
 1. Prob impact matrix - Probability, Impact, Score, Ranking

#### Key Output:  
1. Risk Register
  - Assessments of probability and impacts for each individual project risk
  - Priority level, nominated risk owner

2. Risk Report
  - Prioritized list of all identified risks on the project

### 4. Perform Quantitative Risk
 - process of numerically analyzing the combined effect of identified individual project risks and other sources of uncertainty on overall project objectives

 - Key Benefit- It quantifies overall project risk exposure.

**ITTO (Input, Tools & Techniques, Output)**
| Inputs                                      | Tools & Techniques                     | Outputs               |
|--------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|-----------------------|
| 1. Risk plan                         | 1. Representation of uncertainty                   | 1. Risk report |
| 2. Risk Register, stakeholder register                            | 2. Simulation, sensitivity, decision tree, influence                      |  |

### Tools & Techniques
1. Simulation - model that simulates the combined effects of individual project risks
and other sources of uncertainty to evaluate their potential impact on achieving project objectives

2. Sensitivity Analysis - Sensitivity analysis helps to determine which individual project risks or other
sources of uncertainty have the most potential impact on project outcomes

3. Decision Tree Analysis - Decision trees are used to support selection of the best of several
alternative courses of action

4. Influence Diagram - Influence diagrams are graphical aids to decision making under uncertainty.

#### Key Output:  
1. Risk Report - Assessment of overall project risk exposure, List of prioritized project risks, recommended risk responses

### 5. Plan Risk Responses
 - process of developing options, selecting strategies, and agreeing on actions to address overall project risk exposure, as well as to treat individual
project risks

- **Key Benefit:** identifies appropriate ways to address overall project risk and individual project risks & allocates resources and inserts activities into project
documents and the project management plan as needed

**ITTO (Input, Tools & Techniques, Output)**
| Inputs                                      | Tools & Techniques                     | Outputs               |
|--------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|-----------------------|
| 1. Risk plan                         | 1.  Data Analysis, Expert judgement                   | 1. Change requests |
| 2. Lessons learned register, risk register, risk report                            | 2. overall project risk, contingent response, threat, opporunities                      |  |

Once risks have been identified, analyzed, and prioritized, plans should be developed by the nominated risk owner for addressing every individual project risk the project team considers to be sufficiently important

### Tools & Techniques
1. Strategies for Threats
   - Escalate - When the project team or the project sponsor agrees that a threat is outside the
scope of the project or that the proposed response would exceed the project
manager’s authority

2. Avoid
   - When the project team acts to eliminate the threat or protect the project from its impact

3. Transfer
   - Involves shifting ownership of a threat to a third party to manage the risk and to bear the impact if the threat occurs

4. Mitigate
   - To reduce the probability of occurrence and/or impact of a threat
  
5. Accept
   - Risk acceptance acknowledges the existence of a threat, but no proactive action is taken
  
2. Contingent Response Strategies
   -  For some risks, it is appropriate for the project team to make a response plan that will only be executed under certain predefined conditions
  
#### Key Output:  
1. Risk Register - Agreed-upon response strategies, Specific actions to implement the chosen response strategy
2. Risk Report - Updated to present agreed-upon responses to the current overall project risk exposure and high-priority risks

### 6. Implement Risk Responses
- The process of implementing agreed-upon risk response plans.
- **Key Benefit** - It ensures that agreed-upon risk responses are executed as planned in order to address overall project risk exposure, minimize individual
project threats, and maximize individual project opportunities.

**ITTO (Input, Tools & Techniques, Output)**
| Inputs                                      | Tools & Techniques                     | Outputs               |
|--------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|-----------------------|
| 1. Risk plan                         | 1.  Data Analysis, Expert judgement                   | 1. Change requests |
| 2. Lessons learned register, risk register, risk report                            |                       |  |

### Key Outputs
1. Risk Register - To reflect any changes to the previously agreed-upon risk responses for
individual project risks that are subsequently made as a result of the
Implement Risk Responses process.

2. Risk Report - To reflect any changes to the previously agreed-upon risk response to overall
project risk exposure that are subsequently made as a result of the Implement Risk Responses process

### 6. Monitoring Risk 
- Process of
a. Monitoring the implementation of agreed-upon risk response plans
b. Tracking identified risks
c. Identifying and analyzing new risks &
d. Evaluating risk process effectiveness throughout the project

**Key Benefit** - It enables project decisions to be based on current information about overall project risk exposure and individual project risks.

**ITTO (Input, Tools & Techniques, Output)**
| Inputs                                      | Tools & Techniques                     | Outputs               |
|--------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|-----------------------|
| 1. Risk plan                         | 1.  Data Analysis, Reserve analysis                   | 1. WPI, Change requests |
| 2. Lessons learned register, risk register, risk report                            | 2. Audits, meetings |  |

- Monitor Risks process is carried to determine if:
 1. Implemented risk responses are effective
 2. Level of overall project risk has changed
 3. Status of identified individual project risks has changed
 4. Risk management approach is still appropriate

### Tools & Techniques
1. Technical performance analysis - Compares technical accomplishments during project execution to
the schedule of technical achievement.

2. Reserve analysis - Compares the amount of the contingency reserves remaining to the
amount of risk remaining at any time in the project in order to determine if the remaining reserve is adequate

3. Audits - assess the effectiveness of the risk management process

### Key Outputs
1. WPI - includes information on how project
risk management is performing by comparing the individual risks that
have occurred with the expectation of how they would occur

2. Change Requests - Monitor Risks process may result in a change request to the cost
and schedule baselines or other components of the project management plan
