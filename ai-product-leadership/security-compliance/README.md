# Security & Compliance – AI Product Leadership

This folder (`ai-product-leadership/security-compliance/`) gathers narratives and learning material around **security, privacy, and product‑level compliance** for AI‑product leaders and engineering teams.

---

## What this collection is for

- **Product leaders** shipping AI‑driven, data‑intensive, or IoT‑SaaS products that must comply with regulations like GDPR, ISO 27001, and CE‑related expectations.  
- **Engineering and security leaders** integrating VAPT, OWASP‑secure‑coding, and ISMS‑style practices into SDLC and product design.  

---

## My security‑and‑compliance experience

This collection reflects my hands‑on leadership across:

- **GDPR‑compliance for AI‑product teams**  
  - Led GDPR‑compliance initiatives for a global SaaS product expanding to the EU, embedding **privacy‑by‑design** into product‑specs, DSAR‑workflows, and cross‑border‑data‑transfer safeguards.  
  - Used **GDPR‑PM checklists**, **privacy‑by‑design feedback to engineering**, and **DSAR‑automation** to make compliance repeatable and product‑driven.  

- **ISO 27001‑aligned security in IoT‑manufacturing**  
  - Drove **ISO 27001‑aligned security initiatives** for engineering and product development, partnering with a third‑party security‑as‑a‑service provider in India.  
  - Integrated **security‑into‑the‑SDLC**, defined **OWASP‑aligned secure‑coding rules**, and implemented **CI/CD‑level security‑gates** (e.g., GitHub Actions) for API‑tests and secrets‑management.  

- **VAPT, OWASP, and secure‑coding**  
  - Brought **OWASP‑Top‑10‑aligned secure‑coding** into product‑requirements and PR‑templates (input‑validation, auth, access‑control, crypto, logging).  
  - Instituted regular **Vulnerability Assessment and Penetration Testing (VAPT)** for web‑and‑API‑surface, with findings tracked as user‑stories.  

- **CE‑style product‑safety and risk‑thinking**  
  - Applied **CE‑Marking‑style** risk‑assessment, evidence‑creation, and documentation practices to software‑and‑IoT‑components, aligning cybersecurity with product‑safety expectations.
