# Security & Compliance Training Program – Overview

This document describes a **lightweight security‑and‑compliance training program** for an organisation shipping IoT, web, and API‑based products. It assumes a **product‑leadership** lens: you don’t just attend training, you design and run it.

---

## 1. Target audiences

- **Product‑managers and product‑leaders**  
  - GDPR‑aware‑design, privacy‑by‑design, risk‑thinking.

- **Engineering teams (web, mobile, API, IoT)**  
  - OWASP‑Top‑10, secure‑coding, VAPT‑thinking.

- **Hardware and IoT‑designers**  
  - CE‑style product‑safety, risk‑assessment, and “safety‑plus‑security‑by‑design”.

- **New‑joiners**  
  - Fast‑track security‑and‑compliance induction (1–2 hours).

---

## 2. Training formats

- **Lightning talks** (5–15 minutes)  
  - Delivered in sprint‑planning, all‑hands, or tri‑weekly “security‑coffee” sessions.  

- **Workshops** (45–90 minutes)  
  - Hands‑on:
    - Threat‑modeling sessions.  
    - GDPR‑feature‑design‑workshops.  
    - CE‑risk‑assessment‑workshops for IoT‑products.  

- **Self‑paced materials**  
  - Markdown‑style outlines, risk‑templates, and checklists that people can read independently.

All content is aligned with:
- GDPR‑principles,  
- ISO‑27001‑style expectations,  
- OWASP‑Top‑10,  
- CE‑Marking‑style product‑safety.

---

## 3. Typical calendar (example)

Per quarter, you might run:

- **Q1**
  - “GDPR‑for‑Product‑Managers” lightning talk.  
  - “OWASP‑Top‑10 in 10 minutes” for engineers.  
  - Workshop: “Design a GDPR‑aware feature in 60 minutes”.

- **Q2**
  - “Secure‑coding lightning‑talk” for developers.  
  - Workshop: “Threat‑model your feature using OWASP‑Top‑10”.  

- **Q3**
  - “CE‑Safety‑plus‑Security” talk for IoT‑teams.  
  - Workshop: “CE‑risk‑assessment for a smart‑lock”.

- **Q4**
  - “Security‑&‑compliance recap + roadmap” session.  
  - Review risk‑registers and top‑findings from the year.

This cadence keeps **security‑and‑compliance** top‑of‑mind without overwhelming teams.

---

## 4. How product‑leaders can own this

- **Define the training themes** each quarter as part of your security‑and‑compliance roadmap.  
- **Co‑run workshops** with security‑leads rather than outsourcing them entirely.  
- **Track participation** informally (e.g., “X% of product‑team attended at least one security‑session this quarter”).

By treating training as a **product‑leadership responsibility**, you signal that **security‑and‑compliance are first‑class product‑concerns**, not side‑shows.
