# Data Protection Policy – GDPR‑Aligned

This policy defines how our organisation treats **personal data** in line with **GDPR‑style principles**: lawfulness, fairness, transparency, purpose‑limitation, data‑minimisation, accuracy, storage‑limitation, integrity‑and‑confidentiality, and accountability.

---

## 1. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All **products and services** that process personal data (web, mobile, IoT, internal‑tools).  
- All **employees, contractors, and third‑parties** who design, build, operate, or support these systems.

---

## 2. Core principles

All data‑handling must follow these principles:

- **Lawfulness, fairness, and transparency**  
  - Whenever we collect personal data, we must clearly explain:
    - Why we are collecting it.  
    - How it will be used.  
    - How long it will be retained.  

- **Purpose‑limitation**  
  - Personal data is collected only for **specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes**.  
  - We do not reuse data for other purposes without a fresh consent or legal‑basis‑review.

- **Data‑minimisation**  
  - Collect only the data that is **necessary** for the stated purpose.  
  - Avoid “nice‑to‑have” data‑fields that are not required.

- **Accuracy**  
  - Ensure personal data is accurate and, where necessary, kept up to date.  
  - Provide mechanisms for users to correct or update their data.

- **Storage‑limitation**  
  - Define and enforce **data‑retention periods** for each data‑element.  
  - Automatically delete or anonymise data when it is no longer needed for the original purpose.

- **Integrity and confidentiality**  
  - Protect personal data against unauthorised access, loss, theft, or accidental disclosure.  
  - Apply encryption‑at‑rest and‑in‑transit for sensitive data.

- **Accountability**  
  - Maintain clear documentation of:
    - Data‑flows.  
    - Lawful‑bases (e.g., consent, contractual necessity, legitimate interest).  
    - Data‑subject‑rights‑handling processes.

---

## 3. User‑rights and obligations

We must respect and support user‑rights including:

- Right to be informed  
- Right of access  
- Right to rectification  
- Right to erasure (“right to be forgotten”)  
- Right to restrict processing  
- Right to data portability  

Product teams must design **self‑service flows** (e.g., account settings, consent‑management panels) that allow users to exercise these rights, and systems must be able to execute them without undue delay.

---

## 4. Roles and responsibilities

- **Product‑owners**  
  - Ensure each feature that collects or uses personal data has a documented lawful‑basis and purpose.  
  - Include data‑protection‑considerations in PRDs and design‑reviews.

- **Engineering teams**  
  - Implement privacy‑by‑design and‑by‑default (e.g., default‑opt‑in/off, preference‑stores, audit‑logs).  
  - Follow secure‑coding guidelines to prevent unauthorised‑data‑exposure.

- **Security / Compliance leads**  
  - Lead data‑protection‑impact‑assessments (DPIAs) for high‑risk‑processing.  
  - Coordinate with legal on regulatory‑requests and breach‑notifications.

---

## 5. Incident and breach handling

- Any incident or suspected **data‑breach** must be reported immediately to the security‑and‑compliance team.  
- A **breach‑investigation** must be initiated, including:
  - What data was affected.  
  - How many users were impacted.  
  - What mitigations are in place.  
- If required by law (e.g., GDPR), users and regulators must be notified within the required timeframe.
