# GDPR for Product Managers – Talk Outline

This outline is for a **10–15 minute lightning talk** aimed at product‑managers and product‑leaders. You can run this in a sprint‑planning, all‑hands, or security‑coffee session.

---

## 1. Why GDPR matters for product

- GDPR is not only a legal‑requirement; it’s a **product‑differentiator**:
  - Users trust products that clearly explain:
    - What data is collected.  
    - Why it’s needed.  
    - How it’s protected.  

- For product‑leaders, GDPR means:
  - Thinking in **purpose, lawfulness‑basis, and data‑minimisation** from day one.

---

## 2. Core GDPR principles (very quick)

- **Lawfulness, fairness, transparency**  
  - Be clear about why you collect data.  

- **Purpose‑limitation**  
  - Collect only what you need for that purpose.  

- **Data‑minimisation**  
  - Avoid “nice‑to‑have” fields.  

- **Accuracy**  
  - Give users tools to fix incorrect data.  

- **Storage‑limitation**  
  - Define and enforce retention periods.  

- **Integrity and confidentiality**  
  - Protect data from breaches and misuse.  

- **Accountability**  
  - You must be able to **prove** compliance.

---

## 3. What this means for product‑design

- **PRDs must include GDPR‑sections**:
  - “What data do we collect?”  
  - “What is the lawful‑basis?”  
  - “How long will we keep it?”  
  - “How will the user exercise rights (access, rectification, erasure, portability)?”

- **User‑stories must include privacy‑flows**:
  - “As a user, I can withdraw my consent.”  
  - “As a user, I can delete my account and associated data.”  

- **Design‑reviews must include GDPR‑checks**:
  - “Is this field really necessary?”  
  - “Is this data retention aligned with policy?”

---

## 4. Simple GDPR‑friendly patterns

- **Default‑opt‑out for marketing**  
  - Marketing consent should be explicit and opt‑in; never pre‑checked.  

- **Clear consent‑toggles**  
  - Group related consents (e.g., “marketing”, “analytics”, “improving‑product”).  

- **Self‑service data‑control**  
  - “Download my data”, “Delete my account”, “Edit my profile”.

---

## 5. How to run this as a 10–15 minute session

- **0–2 min** – Why GDPR matters for product and trust.  
- **2–5 min** – Core principles (1–2 sentences each).  
- **5–10 min** – What it means for PRDs, user‑stories, and design.  
- **10–15 min** – Q&A; ask the team to share one GDPR‑question they have today.

You can turn this into a **printed 1‑page cheatsheet** for PMs and drop it into `training/` as well.

---

## 6. Optional follow‑up activity

- In the next sprint, ask each PM to:
  - Add a **GDPR‑section** to one PRD.  
  - Share it in the next all‑hands as a “GDPR‑by‑design” example.

This keeps the training **actionable**, not theoretical.
