# AI Product Leadership: Process Excellence Overview

AI product leadership thrives on structured process excellence to deliver scalable, predictable outcomes in dynamic ML/AI environments. Frameworks like OKR cascades, capacity planning, and governance directly address common AI team pitfalls—ensuring alignment from C-suite strategy to execution.

## Core Process Areas

### 01-operating-model
**OKR cascade + capacity planning | Predictable delivery**

An effective AI operating model defines decision rights, system boundaries, and economic constraints for repeatable AI production. OKR cascading aligns company goals downward while preserving team autonomy—start with 3-5 top-level OKRs, then map team OKRs using shared metrics like model accuracy tied to business KPIs. Pair this with capacity planning via historical velocity data to forecast sprint commitments realistically, targeting 80-90% utilization for predictable delivery and burnout prevention.

### 02-velocity-scaling  
**Team maturity progression | Consistent sprint capacity**

Team maturity progresses through defined stages: 
- **L1**: ±30% velocity variance, ad-hoc dependencies
- **L2**: ±10% variance, structured syncs  
- **L3**: ±3% variance, automated pipelines

Use velocity tracking (story points/sprint) to baseline capacity, aiming for stable output over 6-8 sprints before scaling via hiring or process tweaks like pair programming. Consistent sprint capacity emerges from maturity assessments showing high-performers plateau at sustainable paces. This progression ensures teams handle AI complexities like model retraining without velocity drops.

**[Deep dive → 02-velocity-scaling/README.md]**

### 03-program-governance
**Risk register + planning templates | Zero scope creep**

Maintain a centralized risk register logging AI-specific issues like data drift, ethical biases, or latency degradation—reviewed bi-weekly with mitigation owners assigned. Standardized planning templates (intake forms for features) capture autonomy levels, latency targets, and ROI thresholds to enforce discipline. These practices minimize scope creep to near-zero by tying all changes to OKR impact and requiring council approval for high-risk items.

## Implementation Roadmap

**Pilot Phase**: Roll out OKR cascade + basic risk register in one squad, measuring delivery predictability (90% sprint commitments met).

**Scale Phase**: Quarterly team maturity audits; enterprise-wide template rollout.

**Metrics Dashboard**: Track zero scope creep (change request rejection rates); velocity (burndown charts); OKR attainment.

## 🎯 Leadership Signals
- ✅ Multi-team capacity allocation (20% innovation, 70% delivery, 10% buffer)
- ✅ L1→L3 maturity framework with velocity stability targets
- ✅ Program-level risk governance + production templates
- ✅ Predictable delivery with ±5% weekly velocity variance
